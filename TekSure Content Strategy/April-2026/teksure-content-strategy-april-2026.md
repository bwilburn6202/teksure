### Guide 10: How to Prepare For and Use a Telehealth Video Appointment

**Category:** Health & Wellness Tech
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 15 min
**Target Keywords:** telehealth appointment how-to, video doctor visit seniors, virtual doctor appointment guide, telemedicine beginners, MyChart video visit
**Content Type:** Original
**Roadmap Tie-In:** Expands Health & Wellness Tech Hub (currently only 3 guides — weakest category)

**Step-by-Step Outline:**

1. **What Is Telehealth and When Is It Right?**
   - Video visits, phone visits, patient portal messaging
   - Good for: refills, follow-ups, rashes, cold/flu, mental health
   - Not ideal for: emergencies, physical exams, lab work

2. **What You'll Need**
   - Smartphone, tablet, or computer with camera
   - Stable Wi-Fi connection
   - The app or link from your doctor (MyChart, Doxy.me, Zoom for Healthcare, Teladoc)
   - Quick Tip: Install and test the app the day BEFORE your appointment

3. **Setting Up Your Space**
   - Quiet, well-lit room; camera at eye level
   - Have medications, insurance card, and questions ready
   - Quick Tip: Sit facing a window — natural light is best for video

4. **Joining the Appointment Step by Step**
   - Open link or app at appointment time
   - Grant camera/microphone permission
   - Virtual waiting room — what to expect
   - What to do if doctor can't see or hear you

5. **During the Appointment**
   - Speak clearly, look at camera
   - Show symptoms on camera if needed
   - Ask for instructions in writing via patient portal

6. **After the Appointment**
   - Check patient portal for visit notes
   - Pick up prescriptions (e-prescribing)
   - Schedule follow-up, review insurance claim

7. **Troubleshooting Common Problems**

## Section 7: Aggregation Launch Plan

### Overview

April 2026 is the inaugural month for TekSure's content aggregation system. The goal is to onboard simplified, senior-friendly versions of tech articles from trusted sources, dramatically scaling the content library without proportional writing effort.

### Phase 1: Source Activation (Week 1)

**Tier 1 Sources (activate first):**
- AARP Technology section — highest trust among target demographic
- Senior Planet from AARP — practical how-to articles
- GCFGlobal.org (Goodwill) — free tech tutorials written at basic literacy levels
- Digital.gov — government technology accessibility resources

**Tier 2 Sources (activate Week 2-3):**
- How-To Geek — technically accurate, needs simplification
- Tom's Guide — device reviews and setup guides
- PCMag — beginner-friendly explainers
- NCOA (National Council on Aging) — technology resources

**Not Approved (avoid):**
- Sources with heavy ad loads or affiliate-driven content that could confuse seniors
- Forums or user-generated content without editorial review
- Any source that requires login to access content

### Scraping and Simplification Targets

| Week | Articles Scraped | Articles Simplified & Published | Cumulative Total |
|------|-----------------|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| Week 1 | 10 | 5 | 5 |
| Week 2 | 15 | 8 | 13 |
| Week 3 | 15 | 8 | 21 |
| Week 4 | 15 | 9 | 30 |

### Quality Review Process

Every aggregated article must pass through this pipeline before publication:

1. **Scrape & Extract** — Pull article content via aggregation infrastructure
2. **Simplify** — Rewrite to 6th-grade reading level; TekSure voice and tone; 80%+ original language
3. **Enrich** — Add TekSure-standard elements: difficulty badge, time estimate, screenshots, step numbering
4. **Review** — Manual quality check against TekSure Content Checklist:
   - Is every step actionable and unambiguous?
   - Are screenshots current (not from outdated UI)?
   - Is there proper attribution/source credit?
   - Does it pass accessibility review (alt text, heading hierarchy)?
5. **Publish** — Push live with appropriate category tags and SEO metadata
6. **Monitor** — Track engagement for 7 days; pull or revise underperformers

### Publication Cadence

- **First week:** Publish 1 aggregated article per day (Mon-Fri) to test the pipeline
- **Weeks 2-4:** Ramp to 2-3 aggregated articles per day (Mon-Fri)
- **Never publish aggregated and original guides on the same day** in the same category to avoid self-competition
- **Weekend buffer:** Use Saturdays for quality review backlog; no new publishing on weekends

### Attribution Policy

Every aggregated article must include:
- "Originally adapted from [Source Name]" footer
- Link to original article
- Clear labeling as "TekSure Simplified Guide" to distinguish from original content
