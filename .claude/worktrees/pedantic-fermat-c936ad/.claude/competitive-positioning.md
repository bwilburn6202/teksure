# TekSure Competitive Positioning
**Version 1.0 — March 2026**

---

## One-Line Position

> **TekSure is the only tech help platform built specifically for people who feel left behind by technology — combining free, jargon-free guides with real human support, all in one place.**

---

## The Problem We Solve

When everyday people — seniors, retirees, first-time device owners — hit a tech problem, they have three options:

1. **Google it** — and get 10 results written for developers or power users
2. **Watch YouTube** — and sit through a 12-minute video that assumes they already know the basics
3. **Call a family member** — and feel embarrassed about it again

None of these work for our audience. Google and YouTube were built for people who already speak the language. Paid IT support is expensive, intimidating, and feels like overkill for "my printer stopped working." Family members are patient up to a point.

TekSure fills the gap that none of these options address: **patient, plain-English, step-by-step help designed from the ground up for people who don't consider themselves "tech people."**

---

## Competitive Landscape

### Generic Search (Google, Bing)
**What they offer:** Every answer to every question, indexed from everywhere.
**Why they fall short for our audience:**
- Results are written for general audiences, skewed toward technical users
- No curation for difficulty level or prior knowledge
- No consistent format — every result looks and reads differently
- Zero emotional understanding of the audience's anxiety around technology
- No human backup when search results don't help

**TekSure's edge:** Every guide is written at a defined difficulty level, in a consistent format, with our audience's anxiety explicitly in mind. We write for Carol, not for the average internet user.

---

### YouTube Tutorials
**What they offer:** Free video walkthroughs for almost any tech task.
**Why they fall short for our audience:**
- Videos move at the creator's pace, not the viewer's
- Hard to pause, rewind to a specific step, and follow along simultaneously
- Creators assume prior knowledge and skip "obvious" steps
- No way to search within a video for the specific step you need
- Overwhelming volume — hard to know which video to trust

**TekSure's edge:** Written guides that readers can follow at their own pace, return to at any time, and search precisely. Screenshots at every step. No prior knowledge assumed. Difficulty labeled upfront.

---

### Paid IT Support (Geek Squad, local techs, remote support services)
**What they offer:** A human who fixes your problem for you.
**Why they fall short for our audience:**
- Expensive ($50–$200+ per visit or session)
- Creates dependency — the problem is solved but the person doesn't learn
- Intimidating to book, especially for older adults
- Not available 24/7 for quick questions
- Quality varies wildly

**TekSure's edge:** Free guides that teach people to solve problems themselves, with human support available as a backup (not the first resort). The goal is confidence, not dependency.

---

### Generic "Tech for Seniors" Sites (editorial blogs, library guides)
**What they offer:** Occasional articles aimed at older adults.
**Why they fall short:**
- Inconsistent publishing cadence and quality
- No interactive tools, no bookmarking, no progress tracking
- No human support component
- Often patronizing in tone ("Even grandma can do this!")
- No single authoritative destination — scattered across the web

**TekSure's edge:** A purpose-built platform with 300+ curated guides, interactive tools (Tech Health Quiz, WiFi Troubleshooter, Phishing Scanner), progress tracking, and a consistent, respectful tone. One place for everything.

## The Emotional Job to Be Done

Our audience doesn't just want their tech problem fixed. They want to stop feeling embarrassed, dependent, and anxious about technology.

**The functional job:** "Help me do this thing on my device."
**The emotional job:** "Help me feel like I can handle this myself."
**The social job:** "Help me feel less like the only person who doesn't get this."

Every TekSure guide should deliver on all three — not just the functional one.

---

## Messaging That Wins

When talking about TekSure, these messages resonate with our audience:

✅ **"No judgment"** — validates that feeling lost is normal, not a personal failing
✅ **"Plain English"** — a specific, tangible promise (not vague "simple" language)
✅ **"At your own pace"** — directly addresses the YouTube frustration
✅ **"A patient friend"** — warm, familiar framing without being condescending
✅ **"Free"** — removes the barrier that makes people feel they can't ask for help
✅ **"Real human backup"** — reassurance that they're not alone if the guide doesn't work

---

## Messaging to Avoid

❌ "The best" or "#1" — unsubstantiated, erodes trust with a skeptical audience
❌ "Even beginners can..." — the "even" is condescending
❌ "Simple" — what's simple to one person isn't to another; use "plain English" instead
❌ "Tech for seniors" as a headline — many in our audience don't identify as seniors and find the label patronizing; prefer "everyday people" or "anyone who feels overwhelmed by tech"
