# Video Script: Staying Safe on Social Media
**Slug:** social-media-safety
**Target length:** 75 seconds
**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover

---

## HOOK (5 seconds)

> Is a stranger looking at your Facebook photos right now? They might be — and here's how to stop that in five minutes.

---

## WALKTHROUGH (60 seconds)

> Open Facebook on your phone. Tap your profile photo in the top right corner.

> Now tap Settings and Privacy, then tap Settings. You'll see a long list — scroll down until you see Audience and Visibility.

> Tap Posts. Change "Who can see your future posts?" to Friends only. Tap the back arrow.

> Still in Audience and Visibility, tap Profile Information. Find "Who can see your friends list?" and set it to Only Me. This stops scammers from using your friends list to target people you know.

> Go back one more time and scroll down to "How people find and contact you." Set "Who can look you up using your email address?" to Friends. Do the same for your phone number.

> That's your Facebook locked down. Now let's do Instagram.

> On Instagram, tap your profile photo in the bottom right, then tap the three lines in the top right corner. Tap Settings and Privacy. Tap Account Privacy. Flip on the switch next to Private Account. Now only people you approve can see your posts.

> One last thing — if you ever get a friend request from someone who looks like a friend you already have, don't accept it yet. Check your friends list first. Scammers clone real profiles using real photos to trick the people around you.
