Beginner Guides
These guides cover the essential basics every Windows user should know.
1. How to Turn Your PC On and Off
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: To turn your PC ON: Find the power button. On a laptop, it is usually on the top edge of the keyboard or on the side. On a desktop, it is on the front of the computer tower (a large circle or symbol that looks like a line inside a circle).
Step 2: Press the power button once and let go. You will see lights turn on and the manufacturer's logo appear.
Step 3: Wait for the Windows login screen to appear. Click your username and type your password or PIN.
Step 4: To turn your PC OFF: Click the Start button in the bottom-left corner of the screen (the Windows logo).
Step 5: Click the Power icon (it looks like a circle with a line at the top).
Step 6: Click 'Shut down.' Wait for the screen to go black. Your PC is now off.
Step 7: You can also click 'Sleep' to put the computer in a low-power state (it will wake up faster) or 'Restart' to turn off and back on.
💡 Quick Tip: If your PC is frozen and will not respond, press and hold the physical power button for about 10 seconds until it shuts off. Then press it again to restart.
2. How to Connect to Wi-Fi on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Look at the bottom-right corner of your screen (this area is called the System Tray).
Step 2: Click the Wi-Fi icon (it looks like radio waves or a globe if you are not connected).
Step 3: The Quick Settings panel will open. Click the arrow next to the Wi-Fi icon to see available networks.
Step 4: Find your Wi-Fi network name in the list and click on it.
Step 5: Check the box that says 'Connect automatically' if you want to connect to this network whenever it is available.
Step 6: Click 'Connect.'
Step 7: If the network requires a password, type it in the box that appears. Be careful — passwords are case-sensitive.
Step 8: Click 'Next.' After a moment, you will see 'Connected' under the network name.
💡 Quick Tip: If your network does not appear, make sure your Wi-Fi router is turned on. You can also try clicking 'Show available networks' again or restarting your computer.
3. How to Use the Start Menu
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Click the Windows logo (Start button) at the bottom-center of your screen. This opens the Start menu.
Step 2: At the top, you will see 'Pinned' apps — these are shortcuts to apps you use most. Click any icon to open that app.
Step 3: Below Pinned, you will see 'Recommended' — recent files and apps you have used.
Step 4: Click 'All apps' in the top-right corner of the Start menu to see every app installed on your computer, listed alphabetically.
Step 5: To search for anything: Just start typing while the Start menu is open. Windows will search for apps, files, and settings matching what you type.
Step 6: Your profile picture and the Power button are at the bottom of the Start menu.
Step 7: To pin an app: Right-click it in the All Apps list and choose 'Pin to Start.'
💡 Quick Tip: You can also open the Start menu by pressing the Windows key on your keyboard (between Ctrl and Alt).
4. How to Open and Close Programs
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: To open a program from the Start menu: Click the Start button, then click the program's icon in Pinned or search for it.
Step 2: To open a program from the Taskbar: The Taskbar is the bar at the bottom of your screen. Click any icon in it to open that program.
Step 3: To open a program from the Desktop: If the program has a shortcut on your Desktop, double-click it.
Step 4: To close a program: Click the X button in the top-right corner of the program's window.
Step 5: If a program is not responding: Right-click the program's icon in the Taskbar and click 'Close window.' If that does not work, press Ctrl + Shift + Escape to open Task Manager (more on this in the advanced section).
💡 Quick Tip: To switch between open programs quickly, press Alt + Tab. Hold Alt and press Tab repeatedly to cycle through open windows.
5. How to Use the Taskbar
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: The Taskbar is the bar at the bottom of your screen. It shows your pinned apps and currently open programs.
Step 2: Click any icon on the Taskbar to open or switch to that program.
Step 3: Open programs have a small line under their icon to show they are running.
Step 4: To pin a program to the Taskbar: Open the program, right-click its icon in the Taskbar, and click 'Pin to taskbar.'
Step 5: To unpin a program: Right-click its icon in the Taskbar and click 'Unpin from taskbar.'
Step 6: To rearrange Taskbar icons: Click and drag an icon left or right.
Step 7: The right side of the Taskbar (System Tray) shows the clock, date, Wi-Fi, battery, and sound icons.
💡 Quick Tip: Right-click on an empty area of the Taskbar for options to customize it, including hiding it when not in use.
6. How to Take a Screenshot on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: To capture your ENTIRE screen: Press the Print Screen key (it may be labeled PrtSc or PrtScn on your keyboard). The screenshot is copied to your clipboard. Open a program like Paint or Word and press Ctrl + V to paste it.
Step 2: To capture just the ACTIVE window: Press Alt + Print Screen. This captures only the window you are currently using.
Step 3: To capture a SELECTED area: Press Windows key + Shift + S. Your screen will dim and a small toolbar will appear at the top. Click and drag to select the area you want. The screenshot is copied to your clipboard.
Step 4: To automatically save a screenshot as a file: Press Windows key + Print Screen. The screen will flash briefly. The screenshot is saved in your Pictures > Screenshots folder.
Step 5: You can also use the Snipping Tool: Search for 'Snipping Tool' in the Start menu. It lets you capture areas, windows, or the full screen.
💡 Quick Tip: Windows + Shift + S is the most versatile screenshot method. Use it to capture exactly what you need, then paste it anywhere with Ctrl + V.
7. How to Copy and Paste on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: First, select what you want to copy. For text: Click at the start, hold the mouse button down, and drag to the end. The text will be highlighted in blue.
Step 2: To copy: Press Ctrl + C (hold Ctrl, then press C). The selected content is saved to your clipboard.
Step 3: Click where you want to paste (in another document, email, search bar, etc.).
Step 4: To paste: Press Ctrl + V (hold Ctrl, then press V).
Step 5: To cut (move instead of copy): Press Ctrl + X. This removes the original and puts it on the clipboard.
Step 6: You can also right-click after selecting text to see 'Copy,' 'Cut,' and 'Paste' options in the menu.
💡 Quick Tip: Press Ctrl + Z to undo your last action. If you paste something in the wrong place, just press Ctrl + Z immediately.
8. How to Save a File on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: In most programs, press Ctrl + S to save your work.
Step 2: If this is the first time saving the file, a 'Save As' window will appear.
Step 3: Type a name for your file in the 'File name' field at the bottom.
Step 4: Choose where to save it by clicking a location in the left sidebar: Desktop, Documents, Downloads, etc.
Step 5: Click 'Save.'
Step 6: After the first save, pressing Ctrl + S will save updates to the same file without asking again.
Step 7: To save a copy with a different name: Go to File > 'Save As' and choose a new name and location.
💡 Quick Tip: Get in the habit of pressing Ctrl + S every few minutes while working. This prevents losing your work in case of a power outage or crash.
9. How to Create a Folder on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Open File Explorer by clicking the folder icon in the Taskbar or pressing Windows key + E.
Step 2: Navigate to where you want to create the folder (like Desktop, Documents, or Downloads) using the left sidebar.
Step 3: Right-click on an empty area in the window.
Step 4: Click 'New' then 'Folder.'
Step 5: A new folder will appear with the name highlighted. Type a name for your folder.
Step 6: Press Enter to confirm the name.
Step 7: To put files in the folder, drag and drop them onto the folder.
💡 Quick Tip: You can also create a new folder quickly by pressing Ctrl + Shift + N in File Explorer.
10. How to Change Desktop Wallpaper on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Right-click on an empty area of your Desktop.
Step 2: Click 'Personalize.'
Step 3: Click 'Background.'
Step 4: Under 'Personalize your background,' choose from: Picture, Solid color, Slideshow, or Windows Spotlight.
Step 5: If you chose Picture, click 'Browse photos' to select an image from your computer.
Step 6: Navigate to the image you want, select it, and click 'Choose picture.'
Step 7: Use the 'Choose a fit for your desktop image' dropdown to adjust how the image fills the screen (Fill, Fit, Stretch, etc.).
💡 Quick Tip: Choose 'Slideshow' to have your wallpaper automatically change throughout the day. Select a folder of images and set how often it changes.
11. How to Adjust Screen Brightness on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Method 1 — Quick Settings: Click the battery, Wi-Fi, and speaker icons in the bottom-right corner (System Tray). Drag the brightness slider (sun icon) left for dimmer or right for brighter.
Step 2: Method 2 — Keyboard: Many laptops have brightness keys on the top row of the keyboard (look for sun icons). Press them to adjust.
Step 3: Method 3 — Settings: Click Start > Settings > System > Display. Drag the 'Brightness' slider.
💡 Quick Tip: Turn on 'Night light' in Display settings to reduce blue light in the evening, which can help you sleep better.
12. How to Adjust Volume on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Method 1 — System Tray: Click the speaker icon in the bottom-right corner of the screen. Drag the volume slider left (quieter) or right (louder).
Step 2: Method 2 — Keyboard: Many keyboards have volume keys on the top row (look for speaker icons). Press them to adjust.
Step 3: To mute: Click the speaker icon in the System Tray, then click the small speaker icon to the left of the slider. Click again to unmute.
Step 4: To change output device (speakers vs headphones): Click the speaker icon, then click the arrow next to the volume slider to see available output devices.
💡 Quick Tip: If you have no sound, make sure the correct output device is selected and check that the volume is not muted.
13. How to Use the Search Bar
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Click the Search icon (magnifying glass) next to the Start button, or simply click the search bar if it is visible.
Step 2: Start typing what you are looking for: an app name, a file name, a setting, or even a question.
Step 3: Results appear instantly as you type. They are organized by Best match, Apps, Documents, Web, and more.
Step 4: Click on a result to open it.
Step 5: You can press the Windows key and just start typing — this opens Search automatically.
Step 6: The search also works for calculations (type '125 * 3') and unit conversions (type '10 miles in km').
💡 Quick Tip: Windows Search is the fastest way to open any app or find any file. Press the Windows key, type a few letters, and press Enter.
14. How to Use Microsoft Edge Browser
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Click the Microsoft Edge icon (a blue-green wave shape) in the Taskbar or Start menu.
Step 2: Click the address bar at the top. Type a website address (like google.com) or a search term.
Step 3: Press Enter to visit the website or see search results.
Step 4: To go back: Click the left arrow at the top-left, or press Alt + Left Arrow.
Step 5: To open a new tab: Click the plus (+) icon at the top of the browser, or press Ctrl + T.
Step 6: To close a tab: Click the X on the tab, or press Ctrl + W.
Step 7: To bookmark a page: Click the star icon in the address bar, choose a name and folder, and click 'Done.'
💡 Quick Tip: If you prefer a different browser (like Chrome or Firefox), you can download it from its website and set it as your default browser in Settings > Apps > Default apps.
15. How to Print a Document on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Open the file you want to print.
Step 2: Press Ctrl + P. The Print dialog will appear.
Step 3: Select your printer from the list of available printers.
Step 4: Set the number of copies.
Step 5: Click 'More settings' for additional options like page range, paper size, and orientation.
Step 6: When everything looks correct, click 'Print.'
💡 Quick Tip: If your printer does not show up, make sure it is turned on and connected to the same Wi-Fi network (or plugged in via USB). See the printer connection guide.
16. How to Connect a Printer on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Make sure your printer is turned on.
Step 2: For Wi-Fi printers: Make sure the printer is connected to the same Wi-Fi network as your computer (check your printer's manual for Wi-Fi setup).
Step 3: For USB printers: Plug the USB cable from the printer into your computer.
Step 4: Click Start > Settings > Bluetooth & devices > Printers & scanners.
Step 5: Click 'Add device.'
Step 6: Windows will search for available printers. When yours appears, click on it and click 'Add device.'
Step 7: Windows may download and install necessary drivers automatically.
Step 8: Print a test page to make sure it works: Go back to Printers & scanners, click your printer, and click 'Print test page.'
💡 Quick Tip: Most modern printers are plug-and-play — Windows will recognize and set them up automatically when connected.
17. How to Install Apps from Microsoft Store
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Click the Start button and find 'Microsoft Store' (a shopping bag icon with the Windows logo).
Step 2: Use the search bar at the top to find an app, or browse through categories.
Step 3: Click on an app to see its description, screenshots, and reviews.
Step 4: Click the 'Get' or 'Install' button (for free apps) or the price button (for paid apps).
Step 5: You may be asked to sign in with your Microsoft account.
Step 6: Wait for the download and installation to complete. The app will then appear in your Start menu.
Step 7: You can manage your installed apps by clicking your profile icon in the top-right corner of the Store.
💡 Quick Tip: The Microsoft Store is the safest place to download apps for Windows. Apps are reviewed for security before being listed.
18. How to Update Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Click Start > Settings (the gear icon).
Step 2: Click 'Windows Update' in the left sidebar.
Step 3: Click 'Check for updates.' Windows will search for available updates.
Step 4: If updates are found, click 'Download & install' (some updates will download automatically).
Step 5: Some updates require a restart. When prompted, save your work and click 'Restart now.' Or choose 'Schedule the restart' to pick a more convenient time.
Step 6: Do not turn off your computer during an update. It may take several minutes to an hour.
💡 Quick Tip: Keep your computer updated for the latest security protections and features. Windows Update is set to automatic by default, but it is good to check manually occasionally.
19. How to Restart Your PC
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Click the Start button.
Step 2: Click the Power icon (circle with a line at the top).
Step 3: Click 'Restart.'
Step 4: If you have unsaved work, Windows will warn you. Save your work first, then try again.
Step 5: Your PC will shut down and start back up automatically. Wait for the login screen to appear.
💡 Quick Tip: Restarting your computer fixes many common issues like slow performance, frozen apps, and internet problems. It should always be your first troubleshooting step.
20. How to Check Available Storage on Windows
Difficulty: Beginner


Step 1: Click Start > Settings.
Step 2: Click 'System' in the left sidebar.
Step 3: Click 'Storage.'
Step 4: You will see a bar showing how much space is used on your main drive (usually C:).
Step 5: Below the bar, you will see a breakdown of what is using space: Apps & features, Temporary files, Pictures, Documents, etc.
Step 6: Click any category to see more details and find things you can delete.
💡 Quick Tip: Click 'Temporary files' — this often reveals gigabytes of space you can safely reclaim. Check the boxes for what to remove and click 'Remove files.'

Intermediate Guides
These guides will help you get more out of your Windows PC.
21. How to Set Up Email in Windows Mail
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Note: In Windows 11 (latest versions), the Outlook app has replaced the Mail app. Search for 'Outlook' or 'Mail' in the Start menu.
Step 2: Open the app and click 'Add account.'
Step 3: Choose your email provider (Google/Gmail, Microsoft/Outlook, Yahoo, etc.).
Step 4: Enter your email address and password. Follow any sign-in prompts.
Step 5: Choose what to sync (Mail, Calendar, Contacts) and click 'Done.'
Step 6: Your emails will start downloading. This may take a few minutes.
Step 7: You can add multiple email accounts by going to Settings (gear icon) > Accounts > Add account.
💡 Quick Tip: If you use Gmail, the Outlook app on Windows works very well with it — you do not need to switch email providers.
22. How to Use OneDrive
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: OneDrive is Microsoft's cloud storage — it syncs files across all your devices.
Step 2: OneDrive comes built into Windows. Look for the cloud icon in the System Tray (bottom-right).
Step 3: If you are not signed in, click the icon and sign in with your Microsoft account.
Step 4: Open File Explorer (Windows key + E). You will see 'OneDrive' in the left sidebar.
Step 5: Any file you save in the OneDrive folder is automatically backed up and synced to the cloud.
Step 6: You can access your files from any device at onedrive.com or using the OneDrive app on your phone.
Step 7: Files with a cloud icon are stored online. Files with a green checkmark are available on your computer. Files with a blue sync icon are currently syncing.
💡 Quick Tip: You get 5 GB of free OneDrive storage. Microsoft 365 subscribers get 1 TB (1,000 GB), which is more than enough for most people.
23. How to Set Up Windows Backup
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Click Start > Settings > Accounts > Windows backup.
Step 2: Under 'OneDrive folder syncing,' click 'Manage sync settings' and turn on the folders you want to back up (Desktop, Documents, Pictures).
Step 3: Under 'Remember my apps,' turn this ON so Windows remembers your installed apps if you set up a new PC.
Step 4: Under 'Remember my preferences,' turn this ON to save your settings, passwords, and personalization.
Step 5: For a full system image backup: Search for 'Control Panel' in the Start menu. Go to System and Security > Backup and Restore (Windows 7). Click 'Create a system image.'
Step 6: Choose a location (external hard drive recommended) and click 'Start backup.'
💡 Quick Tip: OneDrive backup is the easiest option for most people — it runs automatically in the background. A full system image backup is useful for recovering from serious problems.
24. How to Use Virtual Desktops
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Press Windows key + Tab to open Task View.
Step 2: At the top, you will see your desktops. Click 'New desktop' (the plus icon) to create another one.
Step 3: You can drag open windows to different desktops by dragging them onto the desktop thumbnails at the top.
Step 4: To switch between desktops: Press Ctrl + Windows key + Left Arrow or Right Arrow.
Step 5: To close a desktop: Hover over it in Task View and click the X. Any open windows will move to the remaining desktop.
Step 6: Each desktop can have its own set of open windows, helping you organize different tasks.
💡 Quick Tip: Use one desktop for work apps and another for personal browsing. This keeps things organized and helps you stay focused.
25. How to Use Snap Layouts
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Snap Layouts let you quickly arrange windows side by side.
Step 2: Hover your mouse over the maximize button (the square icon) in the top-right corner of any window. Do not click — just hover.
Step 3: A grid of layout options will appear. Choose the layout and position you want for this window.
Step 4: Click the position. The window will snap into place.
Step 5: Windows will show your other open windows to fill the remaining spots. Click one to snap it in.
Step 6: You can also snap windows by dragging a window to the edge of the screen: drag to the left for the left half, to the right for the right half, or to a corner for a quarter of the screen.
💡 Quick Tip: Press Windows key + Left Arrow or Windows key + Right Arrow to quickly snap the current window to the left or right half of the screen.
26. How to Customize the Taskbar
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Right-click on an empty area of the Taskbar.
Step 2: Click 'Taskbar settings.'
Step 3: Under 'Taskbar items,' choose which icons appear: Search, Task View, Widgets, and Chat.
Step 4: Under 'Taskbar corner icons,' choose which system icons show in the corner (like pen menu or touch keyboard).
Step 5: Under 'Taskbar behaviors,' you can: Change Taskbar alignment (center or left), Auto-hide the Taskbar, and choose to show badges on Taskbar apps.
Step 6: To change the Taskbar alignment to the left (like older versions of Windows): Under Taskbar behaviors, change 'Taskbar alignment' to 'Left.'
💡 Quick Tip: If you want more screen space, turn on 'Automatically hide the taskbar.' It will slide out of view and reappear when you move your mouse to the bottom of the screen.
27. How to Use File Explorer
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Open File Explorer by clicking the folder icon in the Taskbar or pressing Windows key + E.
Step 2: The left sidebar shows Quick Access (frequently used folders), OneDrive, This PC (your hard drive), and Network.
Step 3: Click 'This PC' to see your hard drives, USB drives, and main folders (Documents, Desktop, Downloads, Music, Pictures, Videos).
Step 4: To navigate folders: Double-click a folder to open it. Click the Back arrow at the top to go back.
Step 5: To search for a file: Click the search box in the top-right corner and type the file name.
Step 6: To change how files are displayed: Click the 'View' menu at the top and choose between Large icons, Details, List, etc.
Step 7: To sort files: Click any column header (Name, Date, Size) to sort by that property.
💡 Quick Tip: Pin your most-used folders to Quick Access by right-clicking the folder and choosing 'Pin to Quick Access.'
28. How to Use Windows Hello (Face/Fingerprint)
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Windows Hello lets you sign in with your face, fingerprint, or a PIN instead of a password.
Step 2: Go to Start > Settings > Accounts > Sign-in options.
Step 3: You will see the available options. Not all computers support face or fingerprint — it depends on your hardware.
Step 4: For Fingerprint: Click 'Fingerprint recognition' and then 'Set up.' Touch the fingerprint sensor with different parts of your finger as instructed.
Step 5: For Face Recognition: Click 'Facial recognition' and then 'Get started.' Look at your camera when prompted.
Step 6: For PIN: Click 'PIN (Windows Hello)' and then 'Set up.' Choose a PIN number (at least 4 digits).
Step 7: Once set up, you can sign in using your chosen method instead of typing your full password.
💡 Quick Tip: A PIN is recommended even if you use fingerprint or face — it serves as a backup and is actually more secure than a password because it only works on that specific device.
29. How to Use the Snipping Tool
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Search for 'Snipping Tool' in the Start menu and open it.
Step 2: Click the 'New' button or press Windows + Shift + S to start a new snip.
Step 3: Choose a mode from the toolbar at the top: Rectangle (draw a box), Freeform (draw any shape), Window (capture a specific window), or Fullscreen.
Step 4: For Rectangle: Click and drag to select the area you want to capture.
Step 5: The screenshot will appear in the Snipping Tool window.
Step 6: Use the toolbar to draw on it, highlight text, or crop it.
Step 7: Click the Save icon (floppy disk) to save it as an image file, or click Copy to copy it to your clipboard.
💡 Quick Tip: You can set a delay in the Snipping Tool (click the clock icon) to capture menus or tooltips that disappear when you click elsewhere.
30. How to Use Clipboard History
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Clipboard History lets you access things you have previously copied, not just the most recent one.
Step 2: To turn it on: Go to Settings > System > Clipboard. Turn on 'Clipboard history.'
Step 3: Copy several items as you normally would (Ctrl + C).
Step 4: To access Clipboard History: Press Windows key + V.
Step 5: A panel will appear showing everything you have recently copied (text, images, links).
Step 6: Click any item to paste it at your cursor location.
Step 7: To pin an item so it stays in your history permanently, click the pin icon next to it.
Step 8: To clear your clipboard history, click the 'Clear all' button at the top of the panel.
💡 Quick Tip: Clipboard History is incredibly useful when you need to copy multiple things from one document to another. Turn it on and start using Windows + V today.
31. How to Connect Bluetooth Devices on Windows
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Put your Bluetooth device (headphones, mouse, keyboard, speaker, etc.) into pairing mode. Check its manual — usually you hold a button until a light blinks.
Step 2: On your PC: Click Start > Settings > Bluetooth & devices.
Step 3: Make sure Bluetooth is turned ON.
Step 4: Click 'Add device.'
Step 5: Click 'Bluetooth.'
Step 6: Windows will search for nearby devices. When your device appears, click on it.
Step 7: Click 'Connect' or 'Pair.' Some devices may ask you to confirm a code.
Step 8: Once connected, the device will appear in your Bluetooth & devices list and will automatically reconnect in the future.
💡 Quick Tip: If the device does not appear, make sure it is in pairing mode, is charged, and is within 30 feet of your computer.
32. How to Use Quick Settings
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Click the battery/Wi-Fi/speaker icons in the bottom-right corner of the Taskbar to open Quick Settings.
Step 2: You will see toggles for: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Airplane mode, Battery saver, Focus assist, Accessibility, and more.
Step 3: Click a toggle to turn a feature on or off.
Step 4: Use the brightness slider (sun icon) and volume slider (speaker icon) to adjust those settings.
Step 5: To customize which toggles appear: Click the pencil icon at the bottom of Quick Settings. Click 'Add' to add new toggles or 'Unpin' to remove ones you do not use.
Step 6: Click anywhere outside the panel to close it.
💡 Quick Tip: Quick Settings is the fastest way to toggle Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and Do Not Disturb on or off without digging through the full Settings app.
33. How to Use Voice Typing
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: Click in any text field where you want to type (like a document, email, or search bar).
Step 2: Press Windows key + H to open Voice Typing.
Step 3: A microphone panel will appear. Click the microphone button to start dictating.
Step 4: Speak clearly and naturally. Windows will transcribe your words in real time.
Step 5: Say punctuation marks out loud: 'period,' 'comma,' 'question mark,' 'new line,' 'new paragraph.'
Step 6: Click the microphone button again (or say 'Stop listening') to stop dictating.
Step 7: Review and edit the text as needed.
💡 Quick Tip: Voice typing works best in a quiet environment. Speak at a natural pace and you will be surprised how accurate it is.
34. How to Compress and Unzip Files on Windows
Difficulty: Intermediate


Step 1: To compress (zip) files: Select the files or folders in File Explorer (hold Ctrl and click to select multiple). Right-click and choose 'Compress to ZIP file.' A new .zip file will be created.
Step 2: Alternatively: Select files, click the three dots (...) at the top of File Explorer, and click 'Compress to ZIP file.'
Step 3: The zip file will appear in the same location. You can rename it by right-clicking and choosing 'Rename.'
Step 4: To unzip (extract) a file: Double-click the .zip file to see its contents. Or right-click the .zip file and choose 'Extract All.'
Step 5: Choose where to extract the files and click 'Extract.'
Step 6: The extracted files will appear in a new folder in your chosen location.
💡 Quick Tip: Zipping files is very useful for emailing multiple files as a single attachment, or for reducing file sizes before sending.

Advanced Guides
These guides cover troubleshooting and advanced configuration.
35. How to Manage Startup Programs on Windows
Difficulty: Advanced


Step 1: Too many startup programs can make your computer slow to boot.
Step 2: Press Ctrl + Shift + Escape to open Task Manager.
Step 3: Click the 'Startup apps' tab on the left sidebar.
Step 4: You will see a list of programs that start when your computer turns on, along with their 'Startup impact' (Low, Medium, High).
Step 5: To disable a program from starting automatically: Click on the program, then click 'Disable' at the top (or right-click and choose 'Disable').
Step 6: This does NOT delete the program — it just prevents it from opening automatically. You can still open it manually anytime.
Step 7: To re-enable a program: Find it in the same list and click 'Enable.'
Step 8: Focus on disabling programs with 'High' startup impact that you do not need immediately when your computer starts.
💡 Quick Tip: Keep essential programs like antivirus enabled at startup. Disable things like game launchers, update checkers, and chat apps you do not need right away.
36. How to Force Close Frozen Programs
Difficulty: Advanced


Step 1: Method 1 — Task Manager: Press Ctrl + Shift + Escape to open Task Manager. Find the frozen program under the 'Processes' tab. Click on it, then click 'End task' at the top.
Step 2: Method 2 — Keyboard shortcut: Press Ctrl + Alt + Delete. Click 'Task Manager.' Then follow the same steps as Method 1.
Step 3: Method 3 — Alt + F4: Make sure the frozen program's window is selected (click on it). Press Alt + F4. This sends a close command to the active window.
Step 4: If the entire computer is frozen: Press Ctrl + Alt + Delete. If you can access the options, click the Power icon and choose 'Restart.' If nothing responds, hold the physical power button for 10 seconds.
💡 Quick Tip: If a program freezes frequently, it may need to be updated, reinstalled, or it may not be compatible with your version of Windows.
37. How to Use System Restore
Difficulty: Advanced


Step 1: System Restore can take your computer back to an earlier point in time if something goes wrong (like after a bad update or driver installation).

38. How to Reset Windows to Factory Settings
Difficulty: Advanced


Step 1: IMPORTANT: This will erase programs and settings. You can choose to keep or remove personal files.
Step 2: Go to Start > Settings > System > Recovery.
Step 3: Under 'Reset this PC,' click 'Reset PC.'
Step 4: Choose one of two options: 'Keep my files' (removes apps and settings but keeps personal files like documents and photos) or 'Remove everything' (completely wipes the computer).
Step 5: Choose 'Cloud download' (downloads a fresh copy of Windows — recommended but requires internet) or 'Local reinstall' (uses existing files on your PC).
Step 6: Review the settings and click 'Next,' then 'Reset.'
Step 7: The reset process will take 30 minutes to over an hour. Your PC will restart several times.
Step 8: When finished, you will see the initial Windows setup screen. Follow the prompts to set up your PC.

39. How to Create a Recovery Drive
Difficulty: Advanced


Step 1: A recovery drive lets you reinstall Windows or troubleshoot your PC even if it will not start normally.
Step 2: You will need a blank USB drive with at least 16 GB of space. All data on it will be erased.
Step 3: Search for 'Create a recovery drive' in the Start menu and open it. Click 'Yes' if asked for permission.
Step 4: Check the box 'Back up system files to the recovery drive.' Click 'Next.'
Step 5: Select your USB drive from the list. Click 'Next.'
Step 6: Click 'Create.' This process may take 30 to 60 minutes.
Step 7: When finished, label the USB drive 'Recovery Drive' and store it in a safe place.
Step 8: To use it: Plug the USB drive into a non-working PC, turn it on, and press the key to enter the boot menu (usually F12, F2, or Delete). Select the USB drive to boot from it.
💡 Quick Tip: Create a recovery drive when your computer is working well. You will be grateful to have it if you ever encounter a serious problem.

41. How to Use BitLocker Encryption
Difficulty: Advanced


Step 1: BitLocker encrypts your entire drive to protect your data if your computer is lost or stolen. (Note: BitLocker is available on Windows 11 Pro, Enterprise, and Education editions.)
Step 2: Search for 'BitLocker' in the Start menu and open 'Manage BitLocker.'
Step 3: Click 'Turn on BitLocker' next to your main drive (usually C:).
Step 4: Choose how to back up your recovery key: Microsoft account (recommended), save to a file, or print it. IMPORTANT: Keep this recovery key safe — you need it if you forget your password.
Step 5: Choose whether to encrypt the entire drive or just the used space (used space is faster for new computers).
Step 6: Choose the encryption mode: 'New encryption mode' for fixed drives in your PC, or 'Compatible mode' for external drives.
Step 7: Click 'Start encrypting.' The process runs in the background and may take a while depending on drive size.
Step 8: Once complete, your drive is encrypted. You will unlock it with your normal Windows login.
💡 Quick Tip: BitLocker is especially important for laptops. If your laptop is stolen, the thief cannot access your files without your login credentials.

43. How to Troubleshoot Slow PC Performance
Difficulty: Advanced


Step 1: Step 1 — Restart: Click Start > Power > Restart. A fresh restart clears memory and temporary issues.
Step 2: Step 2 — Check Task Manager: Press Ctrl + Shift + Escape. Look at the 'Processes' tab. Sort by CPU or Memory to find what is using the most resources. End tasks for programs you do not need.
Step 3: Step 3 — Check storage: Go to Settings > System > Storage. If your drive is nearly full, performance suffers. Delete temporary files and unused programs.
Step 4: Step 4 — Reduce startup programs: In Task Manager, click 'Startup apps' and disable programs you do not need at startup.
Step 5: Step 5 — Check for updates: Go to Settings > Windows Update and install any pending updates.
Step 6: Step 6 — Run Disk Cleanup: Search for 'Disk Cleanup' in the Start menu. Select your C: drive. Check all boxes and click 'OK.'
Step 7: Step 7 — Scan for malware: Open Windows Security (search for it in Start menu) > Virus & threat protection > Quick scan.
💡 Quick Tip: The most common causes of a slow PC are: too many startup programs, low disk space, malware, and needing a restart.
44. How to Use Task Manager
Difficulty: Advanced

49. How to Troubleshoot Wi-Fi Issues on Windows
Difficulty: Advanced


Step 1: Step 1 — Toggle Wi-Fi: Click the Wi-Fi icon in the System Tray. Turn Wi-Fi OFF, wait 10 seconds, turn it back ON.
Step 2: Step 2 — Forget and reconnect: Go to Settings > Network & internet > Wi-Fi > Manage known networks. Click your network and click 'Forget.' Then reconnect and re-enter the password.

Step 7: Step 7 — Update Wi-Fi driver: Search for 'Device Manager.' Expand 'Network adapters,' right-click your Wi-Fi adapter, and click 'Update driver.'
💡 Quick Tip: Try each step one at a time. Most Wi-Fi issues are resolved by the first four steps.

51. How to Transfer Data to a New PC
Difficulty: Advanced

52. How to Set Up Two-Factor Authentication
Difficulty: Advanced


Step 1: Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds an extra security step when signing into your Microsoft account.
Step 2: Go to account.microsoft.com and sign in.
Step 3: Click 'Security' at the top.
Step 4: Click 'Advanced security options.'
Step 5: Under 'Two-step verification,' click 'Turn on.'
Step 6: Follow the prompts. Microsoft will guide you through setting up a verification method: the Microsoft Authenticator app (recommended), a phone number for text codes, or an alternate email.
Step 7: Download the Microsoft Authenticator app on your phone if you chose that option. Scan the QR code shown on screen.
Step 8: Save the recovery code that is provided — store it somewhere safe in case you lose access to your phone.
Step 9: From now on, signing in on a new device will require your password PLUS a code from your authenticator app or phone.
💡 Quick Tip: Two-factor authentication is one of the most effective ways to protect your account. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot get in without the second factor.
