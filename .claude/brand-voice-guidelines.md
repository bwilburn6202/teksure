# TekSure Brand Voice Guidelines
**Version 1.0 — March 2026**
*Generated from brand discovery across Google Drive content library and codebase.*

---

## 1. Who We Are

TekSure is a tech help platform built for people who feel overwhelmed by technology — primarily seniors, retirees, and anyone new to a device or operating system. We explain technology the way a patient, knowledgeable friend would: clearly, warmly, without judgment, and without jargon.

---

## 2. Brand Promise

> **"Tech help that makes sense."**

Every piece of content, every UI label, and every email should deliver on this promise. If a reader has to look up a word we used, we failed.

**Supporting taglines (approved):**
- "Free guides, real human support, and tools built for everyday people."
- "Plain English. No jargon."
- "Step-by-step help for real life."

---

## 3. Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | What to avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Plain English** | Use the simplest word that does the job. "Click" not "navigate to." "Turn off" not "disable." | Jargon, acronyms without explanation, technical shorthand |
| **Patient** | Never assume prior knowledge. Explain context before steps. | "Simply," "just," "obviously," "as you know" |
| **Non-judgmental** | No reader is slow or foolish for not knowing something. Frame from curiosity, not deficit. | "Even a beginner can…" "It's easy, just…" |
| **Empowering** | Every guide ends with the reader more capable than when they started. | Passive framing that positions the reader as helpless |
| **Warm** | Friendly but not slangy. Like a knowledgeable neighbor, not a brand. | Emojis in body copy, exclamation overuse, corporate-speak |
| **Safety-conscious** | We take scams, privacy, and security seriously. Warn early and clearly. | Burying warnings at the end, minimizing real risks |

### Tone Modes

**Guide Mode** (how-to articles, step-by-step guides):
- Calm, directive, reassuring
- Numbered steps, clear actions
- Tip callouts ("Quick Tip:") for helpful shortcuts
- Warning callouts for irreversible actions
- Example: *"Click the Start button in the bottom-left corner of your screen. A menu will pop up…"*

**Alert Mode** (scam alerts, security warnings, safety guides):
- More urgent, protective tone
- Capitalized directives where needed: **NEVER**, **IMPORTANT**, **DO NOT**
- Lead with the risk, then the solution
- Example: *"NEVER give your password to someone who calls or emails you — even if they claim to be from Microsoft or your bank."*

**Marketing/UI Mode** (homepage, CTAs, newsletter):
- Short, punchy, benefit-first
- One clear idea per sentence
- Example: *"Plain English. No jargon. Free."*

**Email/Newsletter Mode** (weekly tips, alerts, welcome):
- Warm and personal
- One topic per email
- Example: *"One friendly email each week — a quick tip, a new guide, or a scam alert worth knowing about."*

## 4. Terminology

### Preferred Terms

| Use this | Not this | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Plain English | Simple language / easy to understand | More specific, more ownable |
| Step-by-step | Tutorial / lesson / walkthrough | Clearer for audience |
| Guides | Articles / posts / content | Consistent with nav and UI |
| Tech help | Tech support / IT support | Friendlier, less corporate |
| Quick Tip | Pro Tip | "Pro" implies expertise our audience may not feel |
| Everyday people | Users / end users | More human |
| Everyday people | Non-technical users | Avoids deficit framing |
| Overwhelmed | Confused / lost / struggling | Validates emotion without insulting reader |
| Verified technician | Expert / professional | Specific trust signal |
| Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced | Easy / Medium / Hard | More respectful difficulty labeling |

### Brand Name Usage
- Always written: **TekSure** (camel case, no space, no hyphen)
- URL/slug format only: `teksure` (lowercase, no space)
- Never: TEKSURE, Teksure, Tek Sure, Tek-Sure

## 5. Content Format Standards

### Guide Structure
1. **Title** — action-oriented, specific ("How to Back Up Your iPhone Photos")
2. **Excerpt** — 1–2 sentences, what you'll learn and why it matters
3. **Difficulty** — Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced (required on all guides)
4. **Read time** — estimated minutes
5. **Steps** — numbered, one action per step, clear expected outcome
6. **Quick Tip callouts** — optional, for shortcuts or helpful context
7. **Warning callouts** — required before irreversible actions

### Callout Labels
- **Quick Tip** — helpful shortcut or bonus context (amber, lightbulb icon)
- **Warning** — irreversible actions, data loss risk, security caution (red/orange)

### Difficulty Taxonomy
| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| Beginner | No prior experience needed. Basic device interaction only. |
| Intermediate | Assumes comfort with the basics. Some settings navigation. |
| Advanced | Multiple steps, system-level changes, or troubleshooting required. |

## 6. Visual Identity

### Colors

| Token | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| `teksure-blue` (light) | `hsl(221, 83%, 53%)` approx. `#1D5FE8` | Primary brand color, buttons, links |
| `teksure-blue` (dark) | `hsl(217, 91%, 60%)` approx. `#3B82F6` | Dark mode primary |
| `teksure-success` | `hsl(142, 71%, 45%)` | Completion states, positive feedback |
| `teksure-warning` | `hsl(38, 92%, 50%)` | Tip callouts, caution states |

### Typography
- **Primary font:** Inter
- **Fallback:** system-ui, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, sans-serif
- **No display or serif fonts** — keep it clean and readable

### Logo
- File: `teksure-logo.svg`
- Always use the SVG — never rasterize for web
- **⚠️ Logo spec not yet documented** — see roadmap item "Logo Usage Spec"

## 7. Emoji Policy

- ✅ Allowed: Newsletter success states, UI confirmation messages (1 emoji max)
- ✅ Allowed: Category/thumbnail emoji in guide cards
- ❌ Never: In guide body copy
- ❌ Never: In warning or alert content
- ❌ Never: More than one per UI element

---

## 8. Things We Never Say

- "It's easy" or "Simply..." — implies anyone who struggles is at fault
- "The #1 resource" or any unsubstantiated superlative — we earn trust, we don't claim it
- "Non-technical users" — deficit framing; use "everyday people" instead
- "Users" in audience-facing copy — use "you" or "people"
- Technical acronyms without explanation on first use (RAM, CPU, VPN, etc.)
