# TekSure Audience Personas
**Version 1.0 — March 2026**

---

## Primary Persona — "Retired Carol"

**Age:** 68 | **Location:** Suburban US | **Device:** iPhone 14 (gifted by her daughter), Windows 11 laptop

### Who She Is
Carol retired two years ago after a career in nursing. She's sharp, curious, and motivated — but technology has always felt like a second language. She didn't grow up with computers and only started using a smartphone in her mid-50s. She loves staying connected with her grandchildren on FaceTime and ordering groceries online, but feels anxious when something unexpected happens on her screen.

### Pain Points
- Panics when she sees an unfamiliar error message or pop-up
- Afraid of accidentally "breaking something" or deleting important files
- Has been targeted by phone scams twice — now distrusts anything that asks for personal information
- Finds YouTube tutorials too fast and assumes too much prior knowledge
- Feels embarrassed asking her kids for help "again"

### Goals
- Feel confident enough to handle basic tech problems on her own
- Understand how to stay safe from scams and fraud
- Keep in touch with family easily (video calls, photos, messages)
- Not feel dependent on others for everyday tech tasks

### How She Uses TekSure
- Finds guides through Google search ("how do I..." on her iPhone)
- Prefers step-by-step guides with screenshots
- Reads at her own pace — doesn't watch videos
- Saves guides to favorites so she can come back later
- Would subscribe to a weekly tip email if it's short and simple

### Content That Resonates
- "How to..." and "What to do when..." framing
- Explicit reassurance ("You won't lose any data by doing this")
- Clear difficulty labels so she knows what she's getting into
- Scam alerts — she forwards these to her friends

### Content That Loses Her
- Guides that assume she knows what "Settings > System > Display" means without showing her how to get there
- Anything that starts with acronyms (RAM, OS, VPN) without explaining them
- Long walls of text without visual breaks

## Secondary Persona — "First-Time Marcus"

**Age:** 47 | **Location:** Urban US | **Device:** Android phone (Samsung Galaxy), just bought a Windows 11 PC

### Who He Is
Marcus works in logistics management and is comfortable with spreadsheets and email, but has always used company-provided IT support. He recently bought his first personal laptop and feels out of his depth outside of work tools. He's not a senior, but he's never had to manage his own device — updates, backups, security settings — and doesn't know where to start.

### Pain Points
- Doesn't know what he doesn't know — overwhelmed by the scope of "getting set up properly"
- Frustrated when guides assume he already understands the file system or how Windows works
- Worried about security: heard too many stories about hacks and viruses
- Feels like tech content is written for teenagers, not adults

### Goals
- Get his new PC set up securely and organized
- Understand what he actually needs to do vs. what's optional or marketing
- Build enough confidence to troubleshoot common problems himself
- Stop feeling like the least tech-savvy person in the room

### How He Uses TekSure
- Searches for specific problems as they come up ("how to back up Windows 11")
- Appreciates intermediate-level content — doesn't need everything explained from scratch
- Willing to read longer guides if they're well-organized
- Would use TekBot to ask quick questions

### Content That Resonates
- "Intermediate" difficulty guides — just above basics
- Practical explanations of *why* something matters (not just how to do it)
- Security and privacy content — this is his biggest concern
- Guides organized by "new PC setup" flow

### Content That Loses Him
- Guides written at a condescending "this is a mouse" level
- Content that's visually cluttered or poorly organized
- Guides that end without explaining what success looks like

---

## Shared Characteristics (Both Personas)

| Trait | Carol | Marcus |
|---|---|---|
| Tech confidence | Low | Low-Medium |
| Motivation | Stay connected, avoid scams | Get organized, stay secure |
| Learning style | Slow, visual, step-by-step | Methodical, context-driven |
| Device ecosystem | Apple + Windows | Android + Windows |
| Support preference | Self-serve guides + human backup | Self-serve first, TekBot for quick Q&A |
| Scam concern | Very high | High |
| Content format | Screenshots, short steps | Longer guides OK if structured |

---

## What Both Personas Need From Every Piece of TekSure Content

1. **No assumed knowledge** — explain every step, including how to get to the setting
2. **Clear expected outcome** — "after this step, your screen should look like..."
3. **No jargon without explanation** — define every acronym on first use
4. **Explicit reassurance** — tell them when an action is safe and reversible
5. **Visible difficulty level** — let them self-select what they're ready for
6. **A way to ask for help** — TekBot, book a technician, or community forum
