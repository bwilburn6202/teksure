The 5-second family trick that defeats voice-clone scam calls

Hi friend,

Here's a scenario more families are facing this spring: the phone rings, it's your grandson, and he sounds upset. He says he's been in a small accident, he's okay, but he needs help — and please don't tell his parents yet.

It sounds exactly like him.

Here's the hard part: it might not be him. Scammers can now copy a voice using just a few seconds of audio from a Facebook video or TikTok. The FTC calls these "voice cloning" calls one of the fastest-growing scams in the country.

The good news? The simplest defense costs nothing.

Pick a family safe word — tonight.

  1. Choose any word. Pancake. Maple. Buttercup. It only matters that no one outside your family knows it.
  2. Tell your family at dinner (or by group text). Five-minute conversation, lasts forever.
  3. If a call ever feels off, ask: "What's our family word?"
  4. If they hesitate, get angry, or change the subject — hang up. You don't owe a scammer politeness.
  5. Then call your loved one back at the number you already have saved.

Why it works: A scammer can clone a voice. They can't guess a word your family picked at the kitchen table.
