## Top 5 Trending Topics for Seniors This Week

### 1. Voice-cloning scam calls (a.k.a. "AI grandchild" scams)
The FTC and AARP have flagged a sharp rise in scam calls that use a few seconds of audio (pulled from social media) to clone a loved one's voice and beg for emergency money. This is the #1 scam complaint among adults 60+ this spring.
**TekSure angle:** Step-by-step "how to verify it's really them" guide. Strong empathy hook ("That sounded just like her…").

### 2. Post-tax-season refund scams
April 15 has passed, but the scam wave hasn't. Fake "IRS refund pending" texts and emails spike for 2–3 weeks after filing. Many seniors second-guess themselves because they actually are waiting for a real refund.
**TekSure angle:** "5 signs the refund text on your phone is fake."

### 3. Apple Intelligence & Gemini on phones — "What is this AI thing on my phone now?"
After the iOS 19 / Android 16 spring updates, seniors are seeing AI summaries, writing helpers, and new Siri/Gemini behavior they didn't ask for. Confusion is high; search volume for "what is Apple Intelligence" and "turn off AI on my phone" is climbing.
**TekSure angle:** Plain-English explainer + how to turn off AI features if you don't want them.

### 4. Mother's Day tech gifts (May 10)
Adult kids are searching for "easy phones for mom," "best tablet for grandma," and "tech gifts for mom over 70." Seniors themselves are searching "how to set up [gift]."
**TekSure angle:** Gift-receiver guides ("So your kids gave you an Echo Show — here's what to do first").

### 5. Spring travel tech — phone use abroad, hotel WiFi safety
Cruise and Europe travel season is starting. Seniors want to know about international roaming, hotel WiFi risks, and using maps offline.
**TekSure angle:** Pre-trip phone checklist guide.
