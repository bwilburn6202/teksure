### Mon Apr 27 · Pillar: Trust + Education

**The phone rings. It's your grandson. He sounds upset.**

He says he's been in a small accident. He's okay, but he needs help — and please don't tell his parents yet.

It sounds exactly like him.

Here's the hard part: it might not be him.

Scammers can now copy a voice using just a few seconds of audio from a Facebook video or TikTok. The FTC says these "voice cloning" calls are now one of the fastest-growing scams in the country.

The simplest defense costs nothing: pick a family safe word. Any word — *Pancake. Maple. Buttercup.* When a call feels off, ask, "What's our family word?"

A scammer can clone a voice. They can't guess a word you set up at the kitchen table.

### Tue Apr 28 · Pillar: Quick Win

**Quick Tip: The 30-second WiFi fix that works 80% of the time.**

When the internet acts up, before you call anyone:

1. Unplug the black box that says "modem."
2. Wait 30 seconds. (Count it out — most people don't wait long enough.)
3. Plug it back in.
4. Wait 2 full minutes for all the lights to come back.

That's it. That's the fix.

If it's still not working, it's probably not you — it's your internet provider. And now you can call them with confidence: "I already restarted my modem."

### Wed Apr 29 · Pillar: Education

**"What is Apple Intelligence and why is my phone asking me about it?"**

If you've updated your iPhone or iPad lately, you've probably seen something new called Apple Intelligence pop up. And nobody really explained what it is.

Here's the plain-English version:

Apple Intelligence is Apple's name for AI tools built into the phone — things like rewriting an email more politely, summarizing a long text thread, or making Siri better at follow-up questions.

You don't have to use it. You can turn the whole thing off in Settings → Apple Intelligence & Siri → toggle off.

You also don't have to feel behind because you haven't tried it. It's one year old. The rest of us are figuring it out too.

### Fri May 1 · Pillar: Trust (Scam Alert)

**Scam alert (real, this week): the "IRS refund pending" text.**

Tax season is over but the scams aren't. The newest wave: a text message that says "Your IRS refund of $1,847 is pending. Confirm your bank info to release: [link]."

Here's the truth: **the IRS will never text you.** Not for refunds. Not for anything.

If you get this text:

1. Don't tap the link.
2. Don't reply STOP (that confirms your number is active — it makes things worse).
3. Forward the text to 7726 (it spells SPAM).
4. Delete it.

That's it. You're done.

→ More scam-defense tips: teksure.com/scam-defense

### Sat May 2 · Pillar: Quick Win

**Saturday morning project: clean up your home screen.**

If your phone screen looks like a pile of laundry, here's a 5-minute fix:

**iPhone:** Press and hold any app → "Remove App" → "Remove from Home Screen." (It still lives in your App Library — you didn't delete it, just tucked it away.)

**Android:** Press and hold any app → drag to "Remove" at the top. Same idea — it's still installed, just off the front page.

Keep only the 8–10 apps you actually use every day. You'll feel 10 years younger every time you unlock your phone.
