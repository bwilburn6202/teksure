## Script

[0–8s] **Hook**
The average investment scam victim in 2026 loses $20,000. And these scams look completely real — professional websites, fake celebrity videos, and convincing account dashboards. Here are the exact warning signs.

[8–22s] **Red flag 1 — Guaranteed returns**
The biggest red flag is a promise of guaranteed high returns with no risk. Safe investments today pay around 4 to 5 percent a year. Anyone promising 10, 20, or 30 percent a month is lying. No exceptions.

[22–36s] **Red flag 2 — Fake celebrity videos**
Scammers now use AI to make videos showing real celebrities like Elon Musk endorsing their platform. These look completely real but are 100% fake. If a social media ad features a celebrity promoting an investment, search the celebrity's name plus "scam" on Google before you do anything else.

[36–48s] **Red flag 3 — The withdrawal trap**
If a platform shows you a big profit but won't let you take out your money without paying a "tax" or "fee" first — that's a second theft. Real investment platforms never charge you a fee to access your own money.

[48–60s] **How to verify**
Before investing anywhere, check two free government websites: investor.gov — that's the SEC's investor education site — and brokercheck.finra.org, where you can look up any financial adviser. If the company isn't listed on either site, walk away.

[60–75s] **Close + call to action**
If you think you've been targeted, call the AARP Fraud Watch helpline free at 877-908-3360. You can also report it to the FTC at ReportFraud.ftc.gov. For the full guide including step-by-step verification instructions, head to TekSure.com — search "investment scam."
