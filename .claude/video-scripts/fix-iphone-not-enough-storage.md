# Video Script: iPhone Says "Not Enough Storage" to Update — Here Is the Fix
**Guide:** fix-iphone-not-enough-storage-to-update
**Target length:** 75 seconds
**Format:** Talking head + iPhone screen recording

---

[HOOK — 0:00–0:08]
"Your iPhone is telling you there's not enough storage to install the latest update — but deleting your photos feels scary. Here's how to make room without losing a single picture."

[EXPLAIN — 0:08–0:18]
"Your iPhone needs between half a gigabyte and two and a half gigabytes of free space to download an update. But your photos are probably taking up most of that space — and you don't have to permanently delete them to free it up."

[FIX 1 — 0:18–0:38]
[Screen recording: Settings → Photos → iCloud Photos]
"First, turn on iCloud Photos. Go to Settings, tap Photos, and turn on iCloud Photos. This uploads your photos to the cloud and replaces the full-size copies on your phone with smaller previews — freeing up gigabytes of space. Your photos are NOT deleted. You can still see every single one in the Photos app."

[FIX 2 — 0:38–0:52]
[Screen recording: Settings → General → iPhone Storage]
"Second, go to Settings → General → iPhone Storage. At the top, iPhone will show you recommendations — things like 'Review Large Attachments' and 'Offload Unused Apps.' Tap each one and follow the steps. This takes about two minutes and usually frees up enough space to update."

[ALTERNATIVE — 0:52–1:05]
"If your phone is still too full, update through a computer instead. Connect your iPhone to a Mac or PC — the computer downloads the update and installs it directly to your phone, no storage needed on the phone itself."

[CLOSE — 1:05–1:10]
"Full step-by-step guide at TekSure.com — link below."
