# Video Script: Tech Support Scams
**Guide:** tech-support-scam-guide
**Duration:** 70 seconds
**Format:** Screen recording + voiceover

## HOOK (0:00–0:05)

> "If you ever get a call from someone saying your computer has a virus — hang up. Here's what's really going on."

[CUT TO: Phone ringing, caller ID shows "Microsoft Support"]

---

## WALKTHROUGH (0:05–0:60)

> "This is called a tech support scam, and it's one of the most common ways people lose money online."

> "Here's the truth: Microsoft, Apple, and Google will **never** call you out of the blue to say your computer has a problem. Never. That's not how they work."

[SHOW: Microsoft logo with big red X over a phone icon]

> "What about those scary pop-ups? If a website ever shows a message like 'YOUR COMPUTER IS INFECTED — CALL THIS NUMBER NOW,' that is also fake."

[SHOW: Screenshot of a fake virus warning pop-up on a browser]

> "Real virus warnings come from your antivirus software — not from a website. To close a fake pop-up, press Alt and F4 on Windows, or Command and Q on a Mac."

[SHOW: Keyboard — Alt+F4 highlighted, then browser closes]

> "Scammers want three things: remote access to your computer, your credit card number, and your personal information like your Social Security number."

[SHOW: Three icons — computer, credit card, ID card]

> "If you already let someone remote in, act fast: disconnect from the internet right now by turning off your WiFi, then run a full security scan."

[SHOW: WiFi settings panel → toggle off → Windows Security scan running]

> "Change your passwords from a different device, and call your bank if you shared any payment information."

[SHOW: Password change screen, then phone call to bank number]

---

## RECAP (1:00–1:10)

> "Remember: no real company will ever call you about a virus. If someone does — hang up, and never give them access to your computer."
