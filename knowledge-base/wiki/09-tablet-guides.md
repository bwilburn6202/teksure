## Guide 1: How to Set Up Your New iPad

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-new-ipad`
**Category:** `phone-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 8 min
**Tags:** ipad, apple, tablet, setup, beginner
**Emoji:** 📱

### Excerpt
Just got a new iPad? Follow these steps to get it connected, personalized, and ready to use.

### Steps

1. **Charge your iPad**
   - Plug in the charging cable and let it charge to at least 50% before starting setup. This makes sure it does not turn off during the process.

2. **Turn it on**
   - Press and hold the top button until you see the Apple logo. Wait for the "Hello" screen to appear.

3. **Choose your language and region**
   - Tap your language (English) and your country (United States).

4. **Connect to Wi-Fi**
   - Select your home Wi-Fi network from the list and enter the password.
   - *Tip:* Your Wi-Fi password is usually on a sticker on the bottom or back of your router.

5. **Set up Face ID or Touch ID**
   - The iPad will ask you to set up facial recognition (Face ID) or fingerprint (Touch ID, on models with a Home button). This lets you unlock the iPad without typing a passcode.
   - *Tip:* You should also set a passcode as a backup. Choose a 6-digit number you will remember.

6. **Sign in with your Apple ID**
   - If you have an iPhone, you probably already have an Apple ID — it is the email and password you use for the App Store. Sign in with the same one to share your apps, photos, and purchases.
   - If you do not have an Apple ID, tap "Create a Free Apple ID" and follow the prompts.

7. **Set up iCloud**
   - iCloud backs up your photos, documents, and settings to the cloud. Turn it on when asked. If you lose or break your iPad, you can restore everything from the backup.

8. **Explore the Home Screen**
   - You will see pre-installed apps like Safari (web browser), Mail, Photos, FaceTime, and the App Store. Swipe left to see more pages. Swipe down from the top-right for Control Center (Wi-Fi, brightness, volume).
   - *Tip:* Make text bigger right away: go to Settings → Display & Brightness → Text Size and drag the slider to the right.

### Official Sources
- [Apple: Set Up Your iPad](https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT210060)

## Guide 2: How to Set Up Your New Android Tablet

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-android-tablet`
**Category:** `phone-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 7 min
**Tags:** android, tablet, samsung, setup, beginner
**Emoji:** 📱

### Excerpt
Unbox your new Android tablet (Samsung Galaxy Tab, Lenovo, Amazon Fire) and get it connected and personalized.

### Steps

1. **Charge the tablet**
   - Plug in and charge to at least 50% before starting setup.

2. **Turn it on**
   - Press and hold the power button on the side or top until the screen lights up.

3. **Select your language and connect to Wi-Fi**
   - Choose "English (United States)" and connect to your home Wi-Fi network.

4. **Sign in with your Google account**
   - Enter your Gmail address and password. This connects you to Google Play Store, Google Photos, Google Drive, and more. If you do not have a Google account, tap "Create account."

5. **Set up screen lock**
   - Choose a PIN, pattern, or fingerprint to protect your tablet. A PIN with at least 6 digits is recommended.

6. **Customize display settings**
   - Go to Settings → Display. Increase the font size and display size for easier reading.
   - *Tip:* On Samsung tablets, go to Settings → Accessibility → Visibility Enhancements → Font Size and Style to make text much larger.

7. **Install apps**
   - Open the Google Play Store. Search for apps you use: Gmail, YouTube, Netflix, Zoom, WhatsApp. Tap "Install."

8. **Learn the basics**
   - **Swipe up** from the bottom to go to the Home screen. **Swipe down** from the top for notifications and quick settings. **Long-press** an app icon to move it or uninstall it.

## Guide 3: How to Use Your iPad or Tablet for Reading (Books, News, Magazines)

**Slug:** `how-to-use-tablet-for-reading`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** reading, books, kindle, news, tablet, ipad, ebooks
**Emoji:** 📚

### Excerpt
Turn your tablet into a digital library — read books, newspapers, and magazines with adjustable text size and built-in lighting.

### Steps

1. **Download a reading app**
   - **Books:** Apple Books (iPad), Kindle app (any device), or Libby (free library books).
   - **News:** Apple News, Google News, or your local newspaper's app.
   - **Magazines:** Apple News+ (subscription) or Zinio.

2. **Get free books from your library**
   - Download the "Libby" app. Sign in with your library card. Browse thousands of ebooks and audiobooks — all free. Books return automatically, so no late fees.
   - *Tip:* If you do not have a library card, most libraries let you sign up online for free.

3. **Buy ebooks**
   - On the Kindle app, buy books through amazon.com in your web browser (not the app). On iPad, use the Apple Books store directly inside the app.

4. **Adjust text for comfortable reading**
   - In most reading apps, tap the middle of the screen, then look for the "Aa" or text settings button. You can change font size, font style, background color (try "sepia" for less eye strain), and line spacing.
   - *Tip:* Reading in dark mode (white text on black background) is easier on the eyes at night.

5. **Use your tablet's accessibility features**
   - Turn on "Speak Screen" (iPad) or "Select to Speak" (Android) to have the device read text aloud to you. Great for when your eyes are tired.

6. **Subscribe to a newspaper**
   - Most major newspapers (The New York Times, The Washington Post, USA Today) have apps with free articles each month. Consider a digital subscription — it is usually cheaper than the print edition.

## Guide 4: How to Use Split Screen on Your iPad or Android Tablet

**Slug:** `how-to-use-split-screen-tablet`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Read Time:** 4 min
**Tags:** split-screen, multitasking, ipad, android, tablet, productivity
**Emoji:** ↔️

### Excerpt
View two apps side by side on your tablet — follow a recipe while watching a video, or read an email while taking notes.

### Steps

1. **iPad: Use Split View**
   - Open the first app. Tap the three dots (···) at the top of the screen. Choose "Split View." Your app slides to the side, and you can pick a second app from the Home Screen.
   - *Tip:* Drag the divider in the middle to adjust how much space each app gets.

2. **iPad: Use Slide Over**
   - This is a smaller floating window on top of your main app. Tap the three dots and choose "Slide Over." Great for quick tasks like checking messages.

3. **Android: Use Split Screen**
   - Open the first app. Tap the recents button (square or swipe up and hold). Tap the app's icon at the top of the preview and choose "Split Screen." Then select the second app.

4. **Useful combinations**
   - Video call + Notes (take notes during a FaceTime or Zoom call)
   - Recipe app + Timer
   - Email + Calendar
   - Web browser + Notes (for research)

5. **Exit Split Screen**
   - iPad: Drag the center divider all the way to one side. Android: Drag the divider to the top or bottom edge.

### Official Sources
- [Apple: Use Split View on iPad](https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT207582)
- [Google: Use Split Screen on Android](https://support.google.com/android/answer/9083702)
