## Guide 1: How to Create and Use Your My Social Security Account

**Slug:** `how-to-use-my-social-security`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 7 min
**Tags:** social-security, ssa, government, benefits, retirement
**Emoji:** 🏛️

### Excerpt
Set up your online Social Security account to check your benefits, get proof of income, and manage your information — without visiting an office.

### Steps

1. **Go to the Social Security website**
   - Open your web browser and type ssa.gov. This is the only official website for Social Security. Do not trust any other address.
   - *Warning:* Scammers create fake Social Security websites. Always check that the address bar shows "ssa.gov" — nothing else.

2. **Click "my Social Security"**
   - On the homepage, find the button or link that says "my Social Security" and click it.

3. **Create an account with Login.gov or ID.me**
   - Social Security now uses Login.gov or ID.me for secure sign-in. You will need your email address, phone number, and a government-issued photo ID (driver's license or state ID).
   - *Tip:* Login.gov is the government's official sign-in service. One account works for Social Security, VA, IRS, and other federal agencies.

4. **Verify your identity**
   - You may need to take a photo of your ID and a selfie. The system compares them to verify it is really you. This protects your account from fraud.

5. **Explore your account**
   - Once signed in, you can view your estimated benefits, check your earnings record, get a benefit verification letter, and request a replacement Social Security card.

6. **Get a benefit verification letter**
   - Click "Get a Benefit Verification Letter" to download an official letter proving your income. This is useful for housing applications, loans, and other situations.

7. **Update your information**
   - If you move or change your bank for direct deposit, you can update this information online instead of visiting an office.

### Official Sources
- [My Social Security](https://www.ssa.gov/myaccount/)
- [Login.gov Help](https://login.gov/help/)

## Guide 2: How to Use Medicare.gov to Manage Your Health Coverage

**Slug:** `how-to-use-medicare-portal`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 7 min
**Tags:** medicare, health-insurance, government, seniors, healthcare
**Emoji:** 🏥

### Excerpt
Log in to Medicare.gov to view your coverage, find doctors, compare plans, and check what is covered — all from your computer or phone.

### Steps

1. **Go to Medicare.gov**
   - Type medicare.gov in your browser. This is the only official Medicare website.

2. **Create or sign in to your account**
   - Click "Log in" and sign in with your Medicare.gov account. If you do not have one, click "Create account." You will need your Medicare card number and personal information.

3. **View your coverage**
   - Once signed in, click "My Coverage" to see your current Medicare plan, what it covers, and when your coverage period ends.

4. **Look up what Medicare covers**
   - Click "What's Covered" and search for a specific test, service, or item (like "flu shot" or "wheelchair"). Medicare will tell you if it is covered and how much you might pay.
   - *Tip:* Many preventive services — like annual wellness visits, flu shots, and some cancer screenings — are covered at no cost to you.

5. **Find doctors and hospitals**
   - Click "Find Care" to search for doctors, specialists, and hospitals near you that accept Medicare.

6. **Compare drug plans**
   - During open enrollment (October 15 – December 7), you can compare Medicare Part D drug plans. Enter your medications and the tool will show you plans ranked by total cost.

7. **Check for scams**
   - Medicare will never call you unsolicited to ask for your Medicare number. If someone calls claiming to be from Medicare and asks for personal information, hang up.
   - *Warning:* Your Medicare card number is as valuable as your Social Security number. Guard it carefully.

### Official Sources
- [Medicare.gov](https://www.medicare.gov)
- [1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227)](tel:18006334227)
- [AARP Medicare Guide](https://www.aarp.org/health/medicare-insurance/)

## Guide 3: How to File Your Taxes Online for Free

**Slug:** `how-to-file-taxes-free-online`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Read Time:** 8 min
**Tags:** taxes, irs, free-file, tax-return, government, finance
**Emoji:** 📋

### Excerpt
File your federal tax return online for free using IRS Free File — no expensive software needed if your income is under $84,000.

### Steps

1. **Check if you qualify for IRS Free File**
   - If your adjusted gross income is $84,000 or less (2025 tax year), you can use IRS Free File. Go to irs.gov/freefile.
   - *Tip:* Even if your income is above the limit, the IRS offers "Free Fillable Forms" — a free electronic version of paper tax forms.

2. **Choose a Free File partner**
   - The IRS partners with tax software companies (like TurboTax, TaxAct, and others) to offer free filing. Browse the list on irs.gov/freefile and select one.

3. **Gather your documents**
   - Before you start, collect your W-2 (from your employer), 1099 forms (for other income), Social Security benefits statement (SSA-1099), and last year's tax return.

4. **Create an account with the tax software**
   - Follow the prompts to enter your personal information, income, and deductions. The software asks questions in plain English and fills in the tax forms for you.

5. **Review your return**
   - Before submitting, carefully review every number. Check your name, Social Security number, bank account info (for your refund), and income amounts.
   - *Warning:* A wrong Social Security number or bank account number can delay your refund by months.

6. **Submit electronically**
   - Click "File" or "Submit." You will get a confirmation that the IRS received your return. Save or print this confirmation.

7. **Track your refund**
   - Go to irs.gov/refunds or use the "IRS2Go" app. Enter your Social Security number, filing status, and refund amount to check the status.
   - *Tip:* E-filed returns with direct deposit are typically refunded in 10–21 days.

### Official Sources
- [IRS Free File](https://www.irs.gov/filing/free-file-do-your-federal-taxes-for-free)
- [IRS: Where's My Refund](https://www.irs.gov/refunds)
- [AARP Tax-Aide (Free tax prep for 50+)](https://www.aarp.org/money/taxes/aarp-tax-aide/)

## Guide 4: How to Renew Your Driver's License Online

**Slug:** `how-to-renew-drivers-license-online`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** dmv, drivers-license, renewal, government, id
**Emoji:** 🪪

### Excerpt
Many states let you renew your driver's license from home — skip the DMV line and get your new license in the mail.

### Steps

1. **Check your state's DMV website**
   - Search for "[your state] DMV online renewal" in Google. Each state has its own rules about who can renew online.
   - *Tip:* Not every renewal can be done online. Some states require an in-person visit every other renewal for a new photo or eye test.

2. **Verify eligibility**
   - The website will ask you to enter your license number and date of birth to check if you qualify for online renewal.

3. **Update your information**
   - Confirm your address and other details. If you have moved, update your address during the renewal.

4. **Pay the renewal fee**
   - Most states accept credit cards, debit cards, or electronic checks. The fee varies by state (typically $20–$50).

5. **Receive your new license**
   - A new license will be mailed to you within 1–3 weeks. Your current license remains valid until the new one arrives.

6. **Consider a Real ID**
   - Starting May 2025, you need a Real ID (marked with a gold star) to board domestic flights. Check if your state offers Real ID upgrade during renewal.
   - *Warning:* Real ID renewals typically require an in-person visit with documents like your birth certificate and proof of address.

### Official Sources
- [USA.gov: Renew Your Driver's License](https://www.usa.gov/renew-drivers-license)
- [Real ID Information](https://www.dhs.gov/real-id)

## Guide 5: How to Register to Vote Online

**Slug:** `how-to-register-to-vote-online`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 4 min
**Tags:** voting, registration, government, elections, civic
**Emoji:** 🗳️

### Excerpt
Register to vote or check your voter registration status online in just a few minutes — most states offer online registration.

### Steps

1. **Go to Vote.gov**
   - Type vote.gov in your browser. This is the official federal website for voter registration.

2. **Select your state**
   - Click your state on the map or choose it from the dropdown menu. You will be directed to your state's voter registration website.

3. **Fill out the form**
   - Enter your name, address, date of birth, and the last four digits of your Social Security number. Some states also ask for your driver's license number.

4. **Submit your registration**
   - Click "Submit" to complete your registration. You should receive a confirmation on screen or by mail.

5. **Check your registration status**
   - You can check whether you are registered at vote.gov or your state's election website. Do this well before Election Day to avoid surprises.
   - *Tip:* Check your registration at least 30 days before an election. Deadlines vary by state.

6. **Find your polling place**
   - On Election Day, go to vote.gov or search "where do I vote" in Google. Enter your address to find your assigned polling location.

### Official Sources
- [Vote.gov](https://vote.gov)
- [USA.gov: Voting](https://www.usa.gov/voting)
