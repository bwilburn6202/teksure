## Guide 1: What Is a Chromebook and Is It Right for You?

**Slug:** `what-is-a-chromebook`
**Category:** `essential-skills`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** chromebook, laptop, chrome-os, google, beginner, buying-guide
**Emoji:** 💻

### Excerpt
A Chromebook is an affordable, easy-to-use laptop that runs on Google's Chrome OS — here is how it compares to Windows and Mac, and who it is best for.

### Steps

1. **What a Chromebook is**
   - A Chromebook is a laptop that uses Google's operating system (Chrome OS) instead of Windows or macOS. It is designed to work mainly through the Chrome web browser and Google apps like Gmail, Google Docs, and YouTube.

2. **What it does well**
   - Email, web browsing, video calls (Zoom, Google Meet), YouTube, Netflix, social media, Google Docs/Sheets/Slides, and light photo editing. It starts up in about 10 seconds and updates automatically.
   - *Tip:* Chromebooks are one of the most secure computers you can buy. Viruses that target Windows do not work on Chrome OS.

3. **What it does not do well**
   - You cannot install traditional Windows programs like Microsoft Office (desktop version), Photoshop, or most PC games. You also need internet access for most tasks, although some apps work offline.

4. **Who it is best for**
   - Anyone who mainly uses their computer for email, web browsing, video calls, social media, and streaming. If you do not need specialized Windows software, a Chromebook at $150–$300 can do everything you need.

5. **How it compares to Windows and Mac**
   - **Price:** Chromebooks start at $150. Windows laptops start around $300. MacBooks start at $999.
   - **Ease of use:** Chromebooks are the easiest to set up — sign in with your Google account and you are ready.
   - **Software:** Windows and Mac run the most software. Chromebooks run web apps and Android apps.
   - **Security:** Chromebooks are the most secure out of the box.

6. **Where to buy**
   - Available at Walmart, Best Buy, Amazon, and Costco. Popular brands: Acer, HP, Lenovo, Samsung, and ASUS.
   - *Tip:* Look for models with at least 4 GB of RAM and 64 GB of storage. A touchscreen is a nice bonus but not required.

## Guide 2: How to Set Up Your New Chromebook

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-chromebook`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** chromebook, setup, chrome-os, google, laptop
**Emoji:** ⚙️

### Excerpt
Get your new Chromebook connected, signed in, and personalized in about 10 minutes.

### Steps

1. **Charge and turn on**
   - Plug in your Chromebook and press the power button (usually in the top-right corner of the keyboard or on the side).

2. **Choose your language and connect to Wi-Fi**
   - Select "English (United States)" and connect to your home Wi-Fi.

3. **Sign in with your Google account**
   - Enter your Gmail address and password. If you do not have one, you can create a free account during setup.
   - *Tip:* Everything on your Chromebook is tied to your Google account — bookmarks, emails, files, and apps. If you already use Gmail on your phone, everything will sync automatically.

4. **Accept the terms and set up Google Assistant**
   - You can enable Google Assistant for voice commands ("Hey Google, open Gmail") or skip this step.

5. **Explore the desktop**
   - The bottom bar (called the "Shelf") works like the Windows taskbar. The circle icon in the bottom-left corner opens the "Launcher" — your app menu. Click any app to open it.

6. **Adjust settings for easier use**
   - Click the time in the bottom-right corner, then the gear icon (Settings).
   - **Larger text:** Settings → Accessibility → Display and Magnification → Change text size.
   - **Larger mouse cursor:** Settings → Accessibility → Cursor and Touchpad → Large mouse cursor.

7. **Install apps**
   - Open the Google Play Store from the Launcher and download apps like Zoom, Netflix, WhatsApp, and Kindle.

## Guide 3: How to Use Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides on a Chromebook

**Slug:** `how-to-use-google-docs-chromebook`
**Category:** `essential-skills`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** google-docs, google-sheets, google-slides, chromebook, office, writing
**Emoji:** 📝

### Excerpt
Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides are free alternatives to Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint — and they work great on a Chromebook.

### Steps

1. **Open Google Docs**
   - Click the Launcher (circle icon), then find and click "Google Docs." Or open Chrome and go to docs.google.com.

2. **Create a new document**
   - Click "Blank" to create a new document, or choose a template (resume, letter, report).

3. **Type and format text**
   - Just start typing. Use the toolbar at the top to make text bold, italic, change the font, or adjust the size. It works similar to Microsoft Word.
   - *Tip:* Everything saves automatically. You never need to press "Save" — look for "All changes saved in Drive" at the top.

4. **Share a document**
   - Click the blue "Share" button in the top right. Enter someone's email address. Choose whether they can "View," "Comment," or "Edit." Click "Send."
   - *Tip:* Sharing is great for collaborating on lists, letters, or family documents. Everyone sees changes in real time.

5. **Use Google Sheets (like Excel)**
   - Go to sheets.google.com. Create a spreadsheet for budgets, lists, or tracking information. Sheets supports formulas, charts, and sorting.

6. **Use Google Slides (like PowerPoint)**
   - Go to slides.google.com. Create presentations with slides, text, images, and transitions.

7. **Access your files anywhere**
   - All your documents are saved in Google Drive. You can access them from any computer, phone, or tablet by signing in to your Google account.

8. **Work offline**
   - In Google Docs, go to the menu (☰) → Settings → toggle on "Offline." This lets you edit documents without internet. Changes sync when you reconnect.

## Guide 4: How to Print from a Chromebook

**Slug:** `how-to-print-from-chromebook`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 4 min
**Tags:** chromebook, printing, printer, wireless
**Emoji:** 🖨️

### Excerpt
Print documents, emails, and photos from your Chromebook to a wireless or USB printer.

### Steps

1. **Make sure your printer is on and connected to Wi-Fi**
   - Your Chromebook and printer need to be on the same Wi-Fi network.

2. **Open what you want to print**
   - Open a document, web page, email, or photo in Chrome.

3. **Press Ctrl + P**
   - This opens the Print dialog. You can also click the three-dot menu (⋮) in Chrome and choose "Print."

4. **Select your printer**
   - Click "Destination" and choose your printer from the list. If you do not see it, click "See More" to search.
   - *Tip:* If your printer does not appear, go to Settings → Advanced → Printing → Printers → Add Printer. Enter your printer's information.

5. **Adjust settings and print**
   - Choose the number of copies, color or black and white, and paper size. Click "Print."

6. **Save as PDF instead**
   - If you do not have a printer, choose "Save as PDF" in the Destination menu. This saves the document as a PDF file you can print later or email to someone.
