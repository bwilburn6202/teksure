## Guide 1: How to Set Up Online Banking

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-online-banking`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 7 min
**Tags:** banking, online-banking, money, finance, checking-account
**Emoji:** 🏦

### Excerpt
Access your bank account from your phone or computer to check your balance, pay bills, and transfer money — safely and securely.

### Steps

1. **Check if your bank offers online banking**
   - Almost every bank in the US does. Look for a "Sign Up" or "Enroll" link on your bank's website, or call the customer service number on the back of your debit card.

2. **Go to your bank's website or download their app**
   - On a computer, type your bank's web address (for example, chase.com, bankofamerica.com, wellsfargo.com). On your phone, search for your bank in the App Store or Google Play Store.
   - *Warning:* Always type the bank's web address yourself. Never click a link from an email or text message claiming to be from your bank — this is a common scam.

3. **Create your online account**
   - Click "Enroll" or "Sign Up." You will need your account number (found on your bank statement or check), Social Security number, and a valid email address.

4. **Choose a strong username and password**
   - Your username should not be your email. Your password should be at least 12 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
   - *Tip:* Use a passphrase like "My-Dog-Likes-Pizza-42!" — long, unique, and memorable.

5. **Set up two-factor authentication (2FA)**
   - This adds a second layer of security. After entering your password, the bank sends a code to your phone via text or an authenticator app. You enter this code to prove it is really you.
   - *Tip:* Always enable 2FA. It is the single best thing you can do to protect your bank account.

6. **Explore the dashboard**
   - You will see your account balance, recent transactions, and options to pay bills, transfer money, and deposit checks.

7. **Set up alerts**
   - Go to your account settings and turn on alerts for large transactions, low balances, and sign-in attempts. These notifications help you spot unauthorized activity quickly.

## Guide 2: How to Deposit a Check with Your Phone

**Slug:** `how-to-mobile-check-deposit`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 4 min
**Tags:** mobile-deposit, check, banking, camera, finance
**Emoji:** 📸

### Excerpt
Deposit a paper check without driving to the bank — your phone's camera does the work.

### Steps

1. **Open your bank's app**
   - Sign in with your username and password.

2. **Find the deposit feature**
   - Look for "Deposit," "Mobile Deposit," or a camera icon. It is usually on the home screen or under the "More" menu.

3. **Endorse the check**
   - Flip the check over and sign your name on the back. Underneath your signature, write "For Mobile Deposit Only" and your account number.
   - *Warning:* Writing "For Mobile Deposit Only" protects you — if someone finds the check, they cannot cash it in person.

4. **Take photos**
   - The app will ask you to photograph the front and back of the check. Place the check on a dark, flat surface. Make sure the entire check is visible and the text is clear.
   - *Tip:* Good lighting makes a big difference. Avoid shadows and glare.

5. **Enter the amount and submit**
   - Type the dollar amount of the check. Double-check it matches what is written on the check. Tap "Submit" or "Deposit."

6. **Keep the check**
   - Hold onto the paper check for at least two weeks after the deposit clears. Then shred it.

## Guide 3: How to Pay Bills Online

**Slug:** `how-to-pay-bills-online`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** bill-pay, utilities, online-banking, autopay, finance
**Emoji:** 💸

### Excerpt
Pay your electric bill, phone bill, and other bills from your computer or phone — no stamps, no checks, no late fees.

### Steps

1. **Decide where to pay**
   - You have two options: pay through your bank's "Bill Pay" feature, or pay directly on the company's website (like your electric company or phone provider).

2. **Set up a payee in your bank**
   - In your bank's online portal, go to "Bill Pay." Add a new payee by entering the company name, your account number with that company, and their payment address (found on your paper bill).

3. **Schedule a payment**
   - Enter the amount and choose a date. You can pay immediately or schedule it for later.
   - *Tip:* Schedule payments 2–3 days before the due date. Electronic payments can take 1–2 business days to process.

4. **Set up AutoPay (optional)**
   - AutoPay automatically pays a bill every month so you never miss a due date. You can usually set this up through the company's website or your bank's Bill Pay.
   - *Warning:* Review your statements each month even with AutoPay. Mistakes and overcharges happen.

5. **Pay directly on a company's website**
   - Go to the company's website (for example, your electric company). Log in or create an account. Find "Pay Bill" and enter your payment method (bank account or card).

6. **Keep digital receipts**
   - After each payment, take a screenshot or save the confirmation email. This is your proof of payment.

## Guide 4: How to Use Apple Pay, Google Pay, or Samsung Pay

**Slug:** `how-to-use-mobile-payments`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** apple-pay, google-pay, samsung-pay, contactless, mobile-payment
**Emoji:** 📱

### Excerpt
Tap your phone at a store checkout to pay — it is faster than swiping a card and more secure too.

### Steps

1. **Check if your phone supports it**
   - iPhone: Apple Pay (built in). Android: Google Pay or Samsung Pay (depending on your phone brand). Look for the app in your phone's app list.

2. **Open the wallet app**
   - iPhone: Open the "Wallet" app. Android: Open "Google Wallet" or "Samsung Wallet."

3. **Add your card**
   - Tap "Add Card" or the "+" button. You can take a photo of your credit or debit card, or type the numbers manually. Your bank will verify the card — you may get a text or call with a code.

4. **Set it as your default payment**
   - Choose which card you want to use most often as your "default." This is the card that will be charged when you tap to pay.

5. **Pay at a store**
   - Look for the contactless payment symbol at checkout (it looks like a sideways Wi-Fi icon). Hold your phone near the terminal. On iPhone, double-click the side button first and use Face ID or fingerprint. On Android, just unlock your phone and hold it near.
   - *Tip:* Mobile payments are actually safer than swiping your card. Your real card number is never shared with the store.

6. **Pay inside apps and websites**
   - Many online stores let you check out with Apple Pay or Google Pay. Look for the button at checkout — it saves you from typing your card number.

## Guide 5: How to Spot and Avoid Banking Scams

**Slug:** `how-to-avoid-banking-scams`
**Category:** `safety-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** scams, banking, phishing, fraud, security, money
**Emoji:** 🚨

### Excerpt
Scammers target bank customers through phone calls, emails, and text messages. Learn how to recognize and avoid the most common banking scams.

### Steps

1. **Know the golden rule**
   - Your bank will NEVER call, text, or email you asking for your password, PIN, or full Social Security number. If someone asks for these, it is a scam — every time, no exceptions.

2. **Watch for phishing emails**
   - Scam emails look like they come from your bank. They say things like "Your account has been locked" or "Verify your information." They include a link to a fake website that looks real.
   - *Tip:* Hover over any link (without clicking) to see the real web address. If it does not match your bank's official website, do not click it.

3. **Beware of phone scams (vishing)**
   - Scammers call pretending to be your bank's fraud department. They may even spoof your bank's real phone number. They create urgency: "Your account has been compromised — we need your PIN immediately."
   - *Warning:* Hang up and call your bank directly using the number on the back of your debit card. Never give information to someone who called you.

4. **Watch for text scams (smishing)**
   - You get a text saying "Suspicious activity on your account — click here." These links lead to fake websites designed to steal your login information.

5. **Use official apps and websites only**
   - Always access your bank through their official app (downloaded from the App Store or Google Play) or by typing the web address directly into your browser.

6. **Report suspicious activity immediately**
   - If you think you have been scammed or notice unauthorized transactions, call your bank immediately. Also file a report with the FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov.
   - *Tip:* The faster you report fraud, the more likely you are to get your money back. Federal law protects you if you report within 60 days.
