## Guide 1: How to Set Up a Wireless Printer

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-wireless-printer`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 7 min
**Tags:** printer, wireless, wi-fi, setup, printing
**Emoji:** 🖨️

### Excerpt
Connect your new printer to your home Wi-Fi so you can print from your computer, phone, or tablet — without any cables.

### Steps

1. **Unbox and plug in your printer**
   - Remove all packaging tape and protective materials. Plug the power cord into the printer and into a wall outlet. Turn it on.

2. **Install the ink or toner**
   - Open the printer's ink compartment and insert the ink cartridges (for inkjet printers) or toner (for laser printers). Follow the colored labels to put each cartridge in the correct slot.
   - *Tip:* Most new printers come with "starter" ink cartridges that have less ink than regular ones. You will need replacements sooner than you might expect.

3. **Load paper**
   - Pull out the paper tray, fan a stack of regular letter-size paper, and place it in the tray. Slide the paper guides so they are snug against the paper.

4. **Connect to Wi-Fi using the printer's screen**
   - On the printer's touchscreen or control panel, find "Settings" or "Network," then "Wireless Setup." Select your Wi-Fi network and enter the password.
   - *Tip:* If your printer has no screen, you may need to use the "WPS" button. Press the WPS button on your router, then press the Wi-Fi button on the printer within 2 minutes.

5. **Install the printer software on your computer**
   - Go to the printer manufacturer's website (hp.com/setup, epson.com/support, canon.com/setup) and download the driver for your model. Follow the installation steps.
   - *Tip:* Windows and Mac often detect the printer automatically. Go to Settings → Printers & Scanners and click "Add Printer."

6. **Print a test page**
   - Open any document or web page. Press Ctrl+P (Windows) or Cmd+P (Mac) to open the print dialog. Select your new printer from the list and click "Print."

7. **Print from your phone**
   - iPhone: Open the document or photo, tap the Share icon, and choose "Print." Your printer should appear if both are on the same Wi-Fi.
   - Android: Open the document, tap the three-dot menu, choose "Print," and select your printer.

## Guide 2: How to Scan a Document with Your Phone

**Slug:** `how-to-scan-document-with-phone`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 4 min
**Tags:** scan, document, camera, pdf, phone
**Emoji:** 📄

### Excerpt
Turn your phone into a scanner — snap a photo of any document and save it as a clean, professional PDF.

### Steps

1. **iPhone: Use the built-in scanner**
   - Open the Notes app. Create a new note, tap the camera icon, and choose "Scan Documents." Hold your phone over the document. The app will automatically detect the edges and take the scan.
   - *Tip:* You can scan multiple pages into one document. When done, tap "Save."

2. **Android: Use Google Drive**
   - Open the Google Drive app. Tap the "+" button, then "Scan." Point your camera at the document. Google Drive will automatically crop and enhance it.

3. **Adjust the scan**
   - After scanning, you can rotate the image, adjust the crop, or apply a filter (black and white often looks best for text documents).

4. **Save and share**
   - Save the scan as a PDF. You can email it, upload it to cloud storage, or print it.
   - *Tip:* Scanned PDFs are great for medical records, insurance documents, and tax forms. Keep digital copies in a folder on your phone or in Google Drive.

5. **Alternative: Use Adobe Scan (free app)**
   - Download "Adobe Scan" from the App Store or Google Play. It automatically straightens pages, removes shadows, and creates clear PDFs.

### Official Sources

- [Google: Scan Documents with Drive](https://support.google.com/drive/answer/3145835)

## Guide 3: How to Print Photos at Home

**Slug:** `how-to-print-photos-at-home`
**Category:** `how-to`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** photos, printing, photo-paper, inkjet, memories
**Emoji:** 🖼️

### Excerpt
Print your favorite phone photos on glossy photo paper — perfect for frames, gifts, and scrapbooks.

### Steps

1. **Buy photo paper**
   - Get glossy or matte photo paper in the size you want (4x6 for standard photos, 8.5x11 for larger prints). HP, Canon, and Epson all sell their own photo paper, or you can buy generic brands.

2. **Load photo paper in your printer**
   - Replace the regular paper in the tray with photo paper. Make sure the glossy side faces the correct direction (check the paper packaging for instructions).

3. **Open the photo on your computer or phone**
   - Find the photo in your Photos app, Gallery, or file explorer.

4. **Print the photo**
   - On a computer: Right-click the photo and select "Print." Choose your printer, set paper size to match your paper, and select "Best" or "High Quality" print quality. Choose "Photo Paper" as the paper type.
   - On a phone: Open the photo, tap Share, then Print. Select your printer and adjust settings.
   - *Tip:* Always select "Photo Paper" or "Glossy" in the paper type setting. Using the "Plain Paper" setting on photo paper will look washed out.

5. **Let the photo dry**
   - After printing, let the photo sit for a minute or two before handling. Ink on photo paper can smudge if you touch it right away.

6. **Alternative: Order prints online**
   - If you do not have a photo printer, apps like Walgreens, CVS, Shutterfly, and Amazon Photos let you order prints from your phone and pick them up at a nearby store — often within an hour.
