## Guide 1: How to Shop Safely on Amazon

**Slug:** `how-to-shop-safely-on-amazon`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 7 min
**Tags:** amazon, online-shopping, safety, delivery, account
**Emoji:** 📦

### Excerpt
Create an Amazon account, find what you need, and order it safely — with tips to avoid scams and counterfeit products.

### Steps

1. **Create an Amazon account**
   - Go to amazon.com and click "Create your Amazon account." Enter your name, email, and a strong password.

2. **Add a payment method**
   - Go to "Your Account" → "Payment options." Add a credit card, debit card, or link your bank account.
   - *Tip:* Use a credit card rather than a debit card for online shopping. Credit cards offer better fraud protection.

3. **Search for a product**
   - Type what you need in the search bar. Be specific — "large-print crossword puzzle book" works better than "book."

4. **Read reviews carefully**
   - Look at the star rating and read the most critical reviews first. Watch out for products with thousands of 5-star reviews but no detailed comments — these may be fake.
   - *Tip:* Check if the product is "Sold by Amazon.com" or a third-party seller. Amazon-sold items generally have better return policies.

5. **Check the price and shipping**
   - Look for "FREE delivery" if you have Prime, or check the shipping cost. Compare prices with other sellers by scrolling down.

6. **Place your order**
   - Click "Add to Cart," then "Proceed to Checkout." Verify your shipping address and payment method. Click "Place your order."

7. **Track your delivery**
   - Go to "Your Orders" to see when your package will arrive. Amazon sends tracking updates by email and through the app.

8. **Return items if needed**
   - Go to "Your Orders," find the item, and click "Return or Replace Items." Most items can be returned within 30 days. You can drop returns off at UPS, Kohl's, Whole Foods, or Amazon Locker.

9. **Avoid scams**
   - Never click links in emails that say "Your Amazon order has a problem." Instead, go directly to amazon.com and check "Your Orders."
   - *Warning:* Amazon will never call you to ask for your password, payment information, or remote access to your computer.

## Guide 2: How to Use Instacart for Grocery Delivery

**Slug:** `how-to-use-instacart`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** instacart, groceries, delivery, food, shopping
**Emoji:** 🛒

### Excerpt
Order groceries from your favorite local store and get them delivered to your door — often within a few hours.

### Steps

1. **Download the Instacart app or go to instacart.com**
   - Create an account with your email and set your delivery address.

2. **Choose a store**
   - Instacart partners with local grocery stores. Select the store you usually shop at (Kroger, Publix, Costco, Aldi, etc.).

3. **Browse and add items**
   - Search for items or browse by category (produce, dairy, snacks). Tap "Add to Cart" for each item you want.
   - *Tip:* Add notes to items if you have preferences: "Bananas — green, not ripe" or "Ground beef — leanest available."

4. **Choose replacements**
   - For each item, you can choose what to do if it is out of stock: suggest a replacement, let the shopper choose, or refund that item.

5. **Select a delivery window**
   - Choose a delivery time that works for you. Same-day delivery is often available. Scheduled deliveries may cost less.

6. **Review and place your order**
   - Check your cart, verify the delivery address, and add a payment method. The total includes the items, a delivery fee, and a service fee.
   - *Tip:* Add a tip for your shopper. They are doing the physical shopping for you and rely on tips as part of their income.

7. **Communicate with your shopper**
   - After your order is placed, a shopper starts selecting your items. They may message you through the app about replacements or questions. Keep your phone nearby.

8. **Receive your delivery**
   - The app shows real-time updates. When the shopper is close, you will get a notification. You can choose "Leave at door" or meet them in person.

### Official Sources
- [Instacart Help](https://www.instacart.com/help)

## Guide 3: How to Use Rideshare Apps (Uber and Lyft)

**Slug:** `how-to-use-uber-lyft`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** uber, lyft, rideshare, transportation, rides
**Emoji:** 🚗

### Excerpt
Get a ride anytime without calling a taxi — Uber and Lyft connect you with nearby drivers through your phone.

### Steps

1. **Download the app**
   - Search for "Uber" or "Lyft" in the App Store or Google Play Store. Both are free to download.

2. **Create an account**
   - Enter your phone number, email, and name. Add a credit card, debit card, or PayPal for payment. Rides are charged automatically — no cash needed.

3. **Enter your destination**
   - Open the app and type where you want to go. The app shows the estimated fare and arrival time before you confirm.
   - *Tip:* You will see the price before you book. If the price seems high, wait 10–15 minutes — prices can drop during less busy times.

4. **Choose your ride type**
   - **UberX / Lyft (standard):** Most affordable, fits 4 passengers.
   - **Comfort / Lyft XL:** More legroom or larger vehicle.
   - **Uber Assist:** Drivers trained to help riders who need extra assistance getting in and out of the car.
   - *Tip:* Uber Assist is a great option for anyone with mobility challenges. The driver will help you to and from the vehicle.

5. **Confirm and wait**
   - Tap "Confirm" to book. The app shows your driver's name, photo, car model, color, and license plate. Wait at the pickup location.
   - *Warning:* Always verify the driver's name and license plate match what the app shows BEFORE getting in the car. Ask "Who are you here for?" The driver should say your name.

6. **During the ride**
   - You can share your ride status with a family member through the app. Tap "Share Trip" to send a live tracking link.

7. **Rate your driver**
   - After the ride, the app asks you to rate the driver (1–5 stars). Payment is charged automatically to your card.

## Guide 4: How to Protect Yourself When Shopping Online

**Slug:** `how-to-protect-yourself-shopping-online`
**Category:** `safety-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** online-shopping, scams, safety, credit-card, fraud
**Emoji:** 🛡️

### Excerpt
Shop online with confidence by following these safety rules — protect your money and personal information from scammers.

### Steps

1. **Only shop on trusted websites**
   - Stick to well-known retailers: Amazon, Walmart, Target, Best Buy. If you find a deal on an unfamiliar website, search for reviews before buying.
   - *Tip:* Check for "https://" in the website address (the "s" means secure). Also look for a padlock icon in the address bar.

2. **Use a credit card, not a debit card**
   - Credit cards have better fraud protection. If someone steals your credit card number, you can dispute the charge. With a debit card, the money is taken directly from your bank account and is harder to recover.

3. **Never shop on public Wi-Fi**
   - Do not enter payment information while connected to free Wi-Fi at coffee shops, airports, or stores. Wait until you are on your home Wi-Fi or use your phone's cellular data.
   - *Warning:* Hackers can intercept data on public Wi-Fi networks. If you must use public Wi-Fi, use a VPN (Virtual Private Network).

4. **Be suspicious of deals that seem too good to be true**
   - An 80% discount on a brand-new iPhone is almost certainly a scam. If the price is dramatically lower than everywhere else, do not buy it.

5. **Check your bank statements**
   - After shopping online, check your credit card and bank statements within a few days. Report any charges you do not recognize immediately.

6. **Use strong, unique passwords**
   - Do not use the same password for your shopping accounts and your email or bank. If a shopping site gets hacked, you do not want attackers to access your other accounts.

7. **Save confirmation emails**
   - Keep every order confirmation and shipping notification. These are your proof of purchase if something goes wrong.
