## Guide 1: How to Set Up and Use Netflix

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-netflix`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** netflix, streaming, movies, tv-shows, entertainment
**Emoji:** 🎬

### Excerpt
Learn how to create a Netflix account, pick a plan, and start watching movies and TV shows on any device.

### Steps

1. **Go to Netflix.com or download the app**
   - On a phone or tablet, open the App Store (iPhone) or Google Play Store (Android) and search for "Netflix." On a computer, open your web browser and go to netflix.com.

2. **Create an account**
   - Tap "Get Started" or "Sign Up." Enter your email address and create a password. Write this down somewhere safe.
   - *Tip:* Use an email you check often — Netflix sends recommendations and billing info here.

3. **Choose a plan**
   - Netflix offers several plans. The "Standard with ads" plan is the most affordable. "Standard" removes ads. "Premium" adds 4K quality if you have a newer TV.
   - *Warning:* You can change your plan anytime, so don't stress about picking the "wrong" one.

4. **Enter payment information**
   - Add a credit card, debit card, or PayPal. Netflix charges monthly and you can cancel anytime with no fees.

5. **Set up your profile**
   - Netflix lets you create separate profiles for each person in your household. Tap "Add Profile" and enter a name. Each profile gets its own recommendations.

6. **Start watching**
   - Browse categories or use the search icon (magnifying glass) to find a specific movie or show. Tap the title, then tap "Play."
   - *Tip:* Tap the "My List" button (+ icon) on any title to save it for later.

7. **Adjust subtitles and audio**
   - While watching, tap the screen, then tap the speech bubble icon. You can turn on subtitles (captions) and change the audio language.

### Official Sources
- [Netflix Help Center](https://help.netflix.com)
- [AARP: How to Use Netflix](https://www.aarp.org/technology/)

## Guide 2: How to Set Up a Roku Streaming Device

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-roku`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 7 min
**Tags:** roku, streaming, smart-tv, setup, entertainment
**Emoji:** 📺

### Excerpt
Get your Roku device connected to your TV and Wi-Fi so you can stream Netflix, Hulu, and hundreds of free channels.

### Steps

1. **Plug in your Roku**
   - Connect the Roku to your TV using the included HDMI cable. Plug the power cable into the Roku and into a wall outlet.
   - *Tip:* If your TV has multiple HDMI ports, note which number you used (HDMI 1, HDMI 2, etc.).

2. **Switch your TV to the right input**
   - Use your TV remote to press the "Input" or "Source" button. Select the HDMI port where you plugged in the Roku.

3. **Follow the on-screen setup**
   - Choose your language, then connect to your Wi-Fi network. Select your network name from the list and enter your Wi-Fi password using the on-screen keyboard.

4. **Create or sign in to a Roku account**
   - You need a free Roku account. You can create one on your phone or computer at roku.com/link using the code shown on your TV.

5. **Add streaming channels**
   - After setup, go to "Streaming Channels" on the home screen. Search for apps like Netflix, Hulu, YouTube, or PBS. Tap "Add Channel."
   - *Tip:* The Roku Channel is free and has thousands of movies and TV shows at no cost.

6. **Start watching**
   - Open any channel from your home screen, sign in with your account for that service, and start streaming.

### Official Sources
- [Roku Setup Guide](https://www.roku.com/en-us/get-started)
- [How-To Geek: Roku Beginner Guide](https://www.howtogeek.com)

## Guide 3: How to Set Up an Amazon Fire TV Stick

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-fire-stick`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** fire-stick, amazon, streaming, alexa, entertainment
**Emoji:** 🔥

### Excerpt
Plug in your Amazon Fire TV Stick and start streaming movies, shows, and free content in minutes.

### Steps

1. **Plug in the Fire Stick**
   - Insert the Fire TV Stick into an HDMI port on your TV. Connect the power adapter to the stick and plug it into a wall outlet.

2. **Switch TV input**
   - Press the "Input" or "Source" button on your TV remote. Select the HDMI port where the Fire Stick is connected.

3. **Pair the remote**
   - The Fire TV remote should pair automatically. If not, hold the Home button for 10 seconds.

4. **Connect to Wi-Fi**
   - Select your Wi-Fi network and enter your password.

5. **Sign in with your Amazon account**
   - If you have an Amazon account, sign in. If not, create one — it's free.
   - *Tip:* If you bought the Fire Stick from Amazon while logged in, it may already be linked to your account.

6. **Download apps**
   - Search for Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, YouTube, or any other streaming service. Select "Get" or "Download."

7. **Use Alexa voice search**
   - Hold the microphone button on the remote and say "Find comedies" or "Play The Office." Alexa searches across all your apps.
   - *Tip:* You can also say "Alexa, what's the weather?" or "Alexa, set a timer for 20 minutes."

### Official Sources
- [Amazon Fire TV Support](https://www.amazon.com/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=G7HXKJSELGRFTCQA)

## Guide 4: How to Use YouTube on Any Device

**Slug:** `how-to-use-youtube`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** youtube, video, streaming, free, entertainment
**Emoji:** ▶️

### Excerpt
Watch free videos, learn new skills, and follow your favorite creators on YouTube — the world's biggest video platform.

### Steps

1. **Open YouTube**
   - On a phone or tablet, open the YouTube app (it's usually pre-installed). On a computer, go to youtube.com in your web browser.

2. **Search for a video**
   - Tap the magnifying glass icon and type what you want to watch. Try "beginner knitting tutorial" or "how to make banana bread."

3. **Play a video**
   - Tap any video thumbnail to start watching. Tap the screen to see playback controls — pause, rewind, or skip ahead.
   - *Tip:* Tap the gear icon (⚙️) to change video quality. Choose "Auto" for the best experience on your internet speed.

4. **Subscribe to a channel**
   - If you enjoy a creator's videos, tap the red "Subscribe" button below the video. New videos from that channel will appear on your Home tab.

5. **Create a playlist**
   - Tap "Save" below a video to add it to a playlist. Playlists let you organize videos by topic — "Recipes," "Exercise," "Grandkids' Favorites."

6. **Turn on captions**
   - Tap the "CC" button on the video player to turn on subtitles. This works on most videos.

7. **Be cautious of ads and scams**
   - YouTube shows ads before and during videos. You can skip most ads after 5 seconds. Never click on ads that promise prizes, free money, or seem too good to be true.
   - *Warning:* Never share personal information in YouTube comments or click suspicious links in video descriptions.

### Official Sources
- [YouTube Help](https://support.google.com/youtube)

## Guide 5: How to Watch Free Movies and TV (No Subscription Needed)

**Slug:** `free-streaming-no-subscription`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** free-streaming, tubi, pluto-tv, roku-channel, entertainment, budget
**Emoji:** 🆓

### Excerpt
You do not need to pay for Netflix or Hulu to watch great movies and TV shows. Here are the best free, legal streaming options.

### Steps

1. **Know your free options**
   - Several services stream movies and shows for free with occasional ads: Tubi, Pluto TV, The Roku Channel, Freevee (by Amazon), and PBS Video.

2. **Download Tubi**
   - Search "Tubi" in your app store or go to tubitv.com. Create a free account with your email. Browse thousands of movies and TV shows.
   - *Tip:* Tubi has a great selection of classic movies, documentaries, and family films.

3. **Try Pluto TV**
   - Pluto TV works like regular cable — it has live channels you can flip through, plus on-demand movies. No account needed.

4. **Use your library card**
   - Many public libraries offer free streaming through apps like Kanopy and Hoopla. Ask your local library if they participate.
   - *Tip:* Kanopy has award-winning films and documentaries. Hoopla has movies, audiobooks, and music — all free with a library card.

5. **Watch PBS for free**
   - The PBS Video app has thousands of free episodes of popular shows like Antiques Roadshow, Nature, and NOVA.

6. **Check YouTube for free movies**
   - YouTube has a "Movies & TV" section with hundreds of free, ad-supported films.

### Official Sources
- [Tubi](https://tubitv.com)
- [Pluto TV](https://pluto.tv)
- [Kanopy](https://www.kanopy.com)
- [Hoopla](https://www.hoopladigital.com)
