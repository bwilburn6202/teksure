Got a "tax refund" text? Read this first.

If you got a text this week saying the IRS owes you a refund, you're not the only one. It's confusing, it looks real, and it can happen to anyone — that's not your fault.

Here's the truth: the IRS will never text you about a refund. If you see one, it's a scam — every time. Look for these four warning signs:

  1. It comes by text or email. The real IRS uses paper mail. Always.
  2. It asks you to "click here" or "verify your info." A real refund needs nothing from you.
  3. It threatens you — saying you'll lose your refund or face arrest if you don't act fast.
  4. The link looks odd. Real IRS pages always end in .gov. Anything like "irs-refund.com" is fake.
What to do instead: Don't click. Don't reply. Forward the text to 7726 (that spells SPAM) — your phone carrier will block the sender. Then delete it.
