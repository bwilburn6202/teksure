## FTC Scam Data (2026)

- **Key findings:** $15.9 billion in fraud losses in 2025, 3 million reports. Imposter scams ranked #1 with $3.5B in losses. Reports from older adults losing $10K+ quadrupled from 2020 to 2024. Key scam tactics include fake fraud alerts, imposter calls using stolen personal data, and AI voice clones. FTC hosted a March 2026 roundtable on top scams affecting older adults.
- **URLs:**
  - https://consumer.ftc.gov
  - https://reportfraud.ftc.gov
- **Added to:** scam-prevention.md

## AARP Tech Trends (2026)

- **Key findings:** Smartphone ownership among adults 50+ rose from 55% (2016) to 90% (2025). Adults 50+ use 14 digital services and 10 apps on average. 81% use tech to engage with the world, 80% for social connections, 65% for finances. 59% of older adults do not believe technology is designed with them in mind. Senior Planet by AARP offers free technology training.
- **URLs:**
  - https://www.aarp.org
  - https://seniorplanet.org
- **Added to:** us-resources.md

## CISA Cybersecurity Best Practices (2026)

- **Key findings:** 4 core practices: strong passwords, software updates, think before clicking, enable MFA. "Secure Our World" tip sheets available in multiple languages. Free resources, technical assistance, and training for individuals and families.
- **URLs:**
  - https://www.cisa.gov/secure-our-world
- **Added to:** password-security.md, us-resources.md

## Google Account Security (2026)

- **Key findings:** 7 essential steps: strong password, 2FA, recovery options, Security Checkup, review connected devices, keep software updated, avoid phishing. Google never asks for passwords via email or phone.
- **URLs:**
  - https://support.google.com
- **Added to:** password-security.md

## Telehealth for Seniors (2026)

- **Key findings:** MyChart is the most common patient portal. 82% of homebound seniors needed help connecting to telehealth. Libraries and community centers may have telehealth booths. Modern platforms are adding larger text, closed captioning, and interpreter services.
- **URLs:**
  - https://telehealth.hhs.gov
  - https://www.ncoa.org
- **Added to:** health-technology.md

## Common Tech Problems Seniors Face (2026)

- **Key findings:** Top issues include forgotten passwords, slow computers, email sync across devices, printer problems, and software updates. Email sync issues are often caused by IMAP vs POP3 misconfiguration. Visual acuity changes after age 50 affect screen readability.
- **URLs:**
  - https://nelowvision.com
  - https://easytechseniors.com
  - https://carevision.com
- **Added to:** device-basics.md
