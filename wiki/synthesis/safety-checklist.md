# Essential Safety Checklist

Use this checklist to make sure you have covered the most important security steps. Work through them one at a time. Check off each item as you complete it.

## Passwords

- [ ] **Use a strong password for every account.** A strong password is at least 12 characters long and includes a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using your name, birthday, or common words.
- [ ] **Use a different password for each account.** If one password is stolen, the others stay safe.
- [ ] **Consider a password manager.** A password manager stores all your passwords in one secure place so you only need to remember one master password.

## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

- [ ] **Turn on 2FA for your email account.** 2FA means you need your password *and* a second step (like a code sent to your phone) to sign in. This makes it much harder for someone else to break in.
- [ ] **Turn on 2FA for your bank and financial accounts.**
- [ ] **Turn on 2FA for social media accounts.**

## Software Updates

- [ ] **Keep your phone updated.** Updates fix security problems. See [[apple]] or [[android]] for how to check for updates.
- [ ] **Keep your computer updated.** See [[windows]] for Windows Update instructions.
- [ ] **Keep your apps updated.** Turn on automatic updates in the App Store or Google Play Store.

## Recognizing Scams

- [ ] **Learn the most common scam types.** Review [[scam-types]] so you know what to watch for.
- [ ] **Never click links in unexpected texts or emails.** Go to the website directly by typing the address into your browser.
- [ ] **Never share passwords, Social Security numbers, or bank details** over the phone, by text, or by email unless you started the conversation with a number you trust.

## Wi-Fi and Network Safety

- [ ] **Protect your home Wi-Fi with a strong password.**
- [ ] **Avoid entering passwords or financial information on public Wi-Fi** (coffee shops, airports). Wait until you are on a trusted network.

## Privacy Settings

- [ ] **Review app permissions on your phone.** Go to Settings > Privacy to see which apps have access to your location, camera, and contacts. Remove access for apps that do not need it.

## Backup

- [ ] **Back up your photos and important files.** Use [[google]] (Google Drive/Photos), [[microsoft]] (OneDrive), or [[apple]] (iCloud) to save copies in the cloud.

## What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

If you think an account has been compromised or you fell for a scam, visit [[us-resources]] for reporting steps and phone numbers.
