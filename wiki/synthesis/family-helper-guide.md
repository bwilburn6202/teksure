# Family Helper Guide

Helping a parent, grandparent, or older relative with technology is one of the most meaningful things you can do. This guide covers how to set up their device, teach them new skills, and support them without frustration on either side.

## Start with Patience

- Go at their pace, not yours. What feels obvious to you may be completely new to them.
- Celebrate progress. Learning to send a text or make a video call is a real achievement.
- Never grab the device out of their hands. Let them tap the buttons while you point and explain.
- Repeat yourself without sounding annoyed. It often takes several tries for something to stick.

## Setting Up Their Device

Before you hand over the phone, tablet, or computer, take care of these basics:

- **Create their main account** ([[google]] for Android, [[apple]] for iPhone, [[microsoft]] for Windows PC).
- **Set a strong password** and write it down in a safe place they can find. See [[safety-checklist]].
- **Turn on two-factor authentication** for their email and bank accounts.
- **Install updates** so the device is current and secure.
- **Increase text size** in Settings > Display or Accessibility. Larger text makes a big difference.
- **Add important contacts** so they can call family with one tap.
- **Remove pre-installed apps** they will never use. A cleaner home screen is less confusing.

## How to Teach Effectively

- **Show, then watch.** Demonstrate a task once, then have them try it while you watch.
- **Write it down.** Create a short, numbered list of steps on paper. Many people prefer a printed reference they can keep by their device.
- **Use their words.** If they call the browser "the internet," use that term.
- **Teach one thing at a time.** Do not try to cover email, video calls, and cloud storage in one session.

## Remote Help Tools

If you do not live nearby, you can still help:

- **Phone or video call** — Walk them through steps while they share their screen or describe what they see.
- **Quick Assist (Windows)** — Built into Windows. You can see and control their screen remotely. Search "Quick Assist" in the Start menu.
- **FaceTime screen sharing (Apple)** — During a FaceTime call, tap the share screen button to see what they see.

## When They Get Scammed

Do not blame them. Scammers are skilled at manipulation and target people of every age. Help them report it through [[us-resources]] and take steps from the [[safety-checklist]] to secure their accounts.
