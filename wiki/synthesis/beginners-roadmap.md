# Beginner's Technology Roadmap

If you are brand new to technology, this six-week plan will walk you through the basics at a comfortable pace. There is no rush — take as long as you need on each week.

## Week 1: Device Basics

Get familiar with your device. Learn how to turn it on and off, charge it, adjust the volume, and find the Home screen. Practice tapping, swiping, and scrolling.

- [[apple]] — If you have an iPhone or iPad
- [[android]] — If you have a Samsung, Pixel, or other Android phone
- [[windows]] — If you have a laptop or desktop computer

## Week 2: Wi-Fi and Email

Connect to Wi-Fi at home or at your local library. Set up an email account so you can send and receive messages.

- [[google]] — Setting up a Gmail account
- [[microsoft]] — Setting up an Outlook account

## Week 3: Web Browsing and Safety

Learn to open a web browser, type in a website address, and search for information. This is also the week to learn what to watch out for online.

- [[scam-types]] — Common scams and how to spot them
- [[safety-checklist]] — Your personal security to-do list

## Week 4: Video Calls and Messaging

Call a friend or family member using FaceTime, Google Meet, or Zoom. Practice sending text messages and photos.

- [[apple]] — FaceTime on iPhone or iPad
- [[google]] — Google Meet for video calls

## Week 5: Cloud Storage and Photos

Learn to take photos, find them in your photo gallery, and back them up to the cloud so they are safe if something happens to your device.

- [[google]] — Google Drive and Google Photos
- [[microsoft]] — OneDrive

## Week 6: Online Shopping and Banking

Learn to make a safe purchase online and check your bank account through your bank's app or website. Always look for the lock icon in your browser's address bar.

- [[safety-checklist]] — Review security steps before shopping or banking online
- [[scam-types]] — Shopping scams and bank text scams to watch for

## After the Roadmap

Keep learning at your own pace. Visit [[us-resources]] for free classes through Senior Planet, Cyber Seniors, and your local library.
