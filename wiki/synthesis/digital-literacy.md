# Digital Literacy for Everyday Life

Digital literacy means knowing how to use technology for everyday tasks — not becoming an expert, but gaining confidence to use technology on your own terms.

## Why It Matters

- 90% of Americans 50+ now own a smartphone (AARP 2026)
- Healthcare, banking, and government services are increasingly online
- Protecting yourself from scams requires understanding how they work

## The Five Core Skills

1. **Using a Device** — Turning on, navigating, opening/closing apps → [[device-basics]], [[smartphone-basics]]
2. **Getting Online** — WiFi, web browsers, searching → [[wifi-networking]]
3. **Communicating** — Email, texts, video calls → [[email-essentials]], [[video-calling]]
4. **Staying Safe** — Scams, passwords, privacy → [[scam-prevention]], [[password-security]]
5. **Solving Problems** — Restarting, updating, asking for help

## Building Confidence

- Start small — one thing at a time
- Practice 5 minutes daily
- Write down steps that work
- Ask for help — family, librarians, community centers
- Be patient — mistakes are part of learning
