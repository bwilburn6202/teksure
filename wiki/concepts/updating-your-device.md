# Updating Your Device

Updates fix security problems, squash bugs, and sometimes add new features. Keeping your device updated is one of the best ways to [[staying-safe-online|stay safe online]].

## Why Updates Matter

- **Security fixes:** Updates close holes that hackers could use to get into your device.
- **Bug fixes:** They repair things that were not working right.
- **New features:** Sometimes you get new tools or improvements.

If you skip updates, your device becomes more vulnerable over time.

## How to Update an iPhone

1. Open **Settings** (the gray gear icon).
2. Tap **General**.
3. Tap **Software Update**.
4. If an update is available, tap **Download and Install**.
5. Enter your passcode if asked and wait for it to finish.

## How to Update an Android Phone

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Scroll down and tap **Software Update** (or **System** > **System Update**).
3. Tap **Check for updates**.
4. If one is available, tap **Download and Install**.

## How to Update a Windows Computer

1. Click the **Start** button (the Windows logo in the bottom-left corner).
2. Go to **Settings** > **Windows Update**.
3. Click **Check for updates**.
4. If updates are found, click **Download & install**.

## Before You Update

- **Plug in your device** so the battery does not die during the update.
- **Connect to Wi-Fi** so you do not use up your mobile data.
- **Save your work** on a computer before starting.
- Updates can take anywhere from a few minutes to an hour. Your device may restart during this time. That is normal.

**Quick Tip:** Turn on **Automatic Updates** in your device settings so you do not have to remember to check.
