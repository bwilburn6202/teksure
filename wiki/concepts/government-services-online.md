# Government Services Online

Many government services that used to require a trip to an office or a phone call can now be done from your computer or phone. Here are the most important ones for seniors.

## Social Security (ssa.gov)

Create a free "my Social Security" account to:
- Check your benefit amount
- Change your address or direct deposit
- Get a replacement Social Security card
- Download tax forms (SSA-1099)

You will need your Social Security number, a valid email address, and a US mailing address to sign up.

## Medicare (medicare.gov)

Log in to medicare.gov to:
- See what your plan covers
- Compare Medicare Advantage and drug plans
- Find doctors and hospitals that accept Medicare
- Check your claims and costs

During open enrollment (October 15 to December 7), you can switch plans online.

## Taxes (irs.gov)

- **IRS Free File** — if your income is under $84,000, you can file your federal taxes free at irs.gov/freefile
- **AARP Tax-Aide** — free tax preparation help for people 50 and older (aarpfoundation.org/taxaide)
- **Where's My Refund** — check your refund status at irs.gov/refunds

## Veterans Benefits (va.gov)

Veterans can use va.gov to:
- Apply for VA health care
- Check disability claim status
- Refill prescriptions
- Send secure messages to your VA doctor

## Benefits Finder (benefits.gov)

Not sure what help is available? Visit benefits.gov and answer a few questions. The site will show you federal and state programs you may qualify for, including food assistance (SNAP), energy assistance (LIHEAP), and housing help.

## Staying Safe on Government Websites

- **Real government websites end in .gov** — if a website asks for payment and does not end in .gov, it may be a scam.
- **Government agencies will never call and demand immediate payment** or threaten arrest.
- **Never share your Social Security number** by email or text message.
