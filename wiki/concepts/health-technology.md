# Health Technology

## Technology Can Help You Stay Healthy

More and more health services are available online and through apps. You do not need to be a tech expert to take advantage of them. This guide covers the most common health-related tools and how to get started.

## Patient Portals (Like MyChart)

A patient portal is a secure website or app where you can:

- **View test results** as soon as your doctor releases them.
- **Send messages** to your doctor's office without calling.
- **Request prescription refills.**
- **See upcoming appointments** and schedule new ones.

**MyChart** is the most widely used patient portal in the United States. To set up your account:

1. Ask your doctor's office for a MyChart activation code.
2. Download the **MyChart** app or visit **mychart.com**.
3. Create your account using the activation code, your email, and a strong password.

## Telehealth Appointments

Telehealth means visiting your doctor by video call instead of going to the office. It is great for follow-ups, minor concerns, and when getting to the office is difficult.

1. Your doctor's office will send you a link by email or text.
2. At your appointment time, tap the link to join the video call.
3. Make sure your camera, microphone, and internet connection are working beforehand (see [[video-calling]] for help).

Many insurance plans, including Medicare, cover telehealth visits.

## Fitness Trackers and Smartwatches

Devices like the **Apple Watch**, **Fitbit**, and **Samsung Galaxy Watch** can track:

- Steps and distance walked.
- Heart rate throughout the day.
- Sleep patterns.
- Reminders to move if you have been sitting too long.

These devices sync with an app on your phone where you can view your health trends over time.

## Medication Reminder Apps

Forgetting to take medication is common. These free apps can send you reminders:

- **Medisafe** — Set up your medications and get alerts when it is time to take each one.
- **CareZone** — Track medications, keep notes, and share information with family or caregivers.
- **Apple Health / Google Health** — Built-in health apps on your phone can also store medication schedules.

## Medicare Online

You can manage your Medicare benefits at **Medicare.gov**:

- View your coverage and plans.
- Find doctors and hospitals that accept Medicare.
- Check claims and see what Medicare has paid.

Create an account at **Medicare.gov** to get started. You will need your Medicare number and a valid email address.
