# Tablets

A tablet is a portable touchscreen device — bigger than a phone but smaller and lighter than a laptop. Tablets are popular for reading, video calls, watching shows, browsing the internet, and playing games.

## iPad vs Android Tablets vs Kindle

- **iPad** (Apple) — the most popular tablet. Works with the App Store, FaceTime, and iCloud. Starts around $329. Best if you already use an iPhone.
- **Android tablets** (Samsung, Lenovo, Amazon) — use the Google Play Store. More variety in size and price. Samsung Galaxy Tab is the most popular. Starts around $150.
- **Amazon Fire tablet** — the most affordable option (starts around $60). Works best with Amazon content (Kindle books, Prime Video). Has a simpler app selection.
- **Kindle e-reader** — designed only for reading books. The screen looks like paper, which is easier on the eyes. Not a full tablet.

## When to Use a Tablet vs Phone vs Computer

| Task | Best Device |
|------|------------|
| Reading books or news | Tablet or Kindle |
| Video calls with family | Tablet (bigger screen than phone) |
| Watching shows in bed | Tablet |
| Writing long emails or documents | Computer (has a keyboard) |
| Quick texts and phone calls | Phone |
| Browsing the web on the couch | Tablet |

## Getting Started With a Tablet

1. **Charge it fully** before first use.
2. **Connect to Wi-Fi** — tablets need an internet connection for most tasks.
3. **Sign in** with your Apple Account (iPad) or Google Account (Android). This lets you download apps and back up your data.
4. **Make the text bigger** if needed — go to Settings > Display > Text Size.
5. **Download a few apps** to start: a web browser, email, video calling (Zoom or FaceTime), and a streaming app.

## Accessibility Features

Most tablets have built-in features to help with vision, hearing, and motor challenges:
- **Text size** — make all text larger
- **Bold text** — makes text thicker and clearer
- **Zoom** — magnify any part of the screen
- **Voice control** — speak commands instead of tapping
- **Screen reader** — the tablet reads everything aloud
