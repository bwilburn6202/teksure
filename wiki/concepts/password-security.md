# Password Security

## Why Passwords Matter

Your passwords are the locks on your digital life. A weak password is like leaving your front door wide open. Taking a few minutes to improve your passwords can protect your bank accounts, email, and personal information.

## Creating a Strong Password

A good password has three qualities: it is **long**, **unique**, and **hard to guess**.

- Use at least 12 characters.
- Mix uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
- **Never** use your name, birthday, pet's name, or "password123."
- A helpful trick: pick a sentence you can remember, like "My dog Buster loves 3 walks!" — that makes a strong password.

## What Is a Password Manager?

A password manager is an app that remembers all your passwords for you. You only need to remember one master password to unlock it. Popular options include:

- **Apple Keychain** (built into iPhones and Macs)
- **Google Password Manager** (built into Chrome and Android)
- **1Password** or **Bitwarden** (work on all devices)

These tools can also create strong passwords for you automatically.

## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds a second lock to your account. After typing your password, you also enter a short code sent to your phone by text message or an app. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot get in without that code.

Turn on 2FA for your most important accounts: email, banking, and social media.

## What to Do If You Think You Have Been Hacked

1. **Change your password immediately** on the affected account.
2. Change the password on any other account where you used the same password.
3. Turn on two-factor authentication.
4. Check your bank and credit card statements for charges you do not recognize.
5. Report identity theft at **IdentityTheft.gov**.

## Resetting a Forgotten Password

Every login page has a "Forgot Password?" link. Tap it, enter your email address, and check your inbox for a reset link. If you do not see the email, check your spam or junk folder.
