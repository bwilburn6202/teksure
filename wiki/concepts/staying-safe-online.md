# Staying Safe Online

The internet is a wonderful tool, but you need to protect yourself. Here are the five most important safety rules.

## 1. Do Not Click Links from Strangers

If you get a text, email, or message from someone you do not know — do not tap any links in it. Scammers send fake messages that look real to trick you into giving up personal information. When in doubt, delete it.

This includes messages that say your account is locked, you won a prize, or you owe money. Real companies will not ask you to fix problems through a text message link.

## 2. Use Different Passwords

Do not use the same password for everything. If a hacker gets one password, they can break into all your accounts. Use a different [[password-security|password]] for each important account — especially email, banking, and social media.

**Quick Tip:** Write your passwords down in a notebook you keep in a safe place at home. That is better than reusing the same password everywhere.

## 3. Turn On Two-Factor Authentication

Two-factor authentication (also called 2FA) adds a second layer of protection. After you type your password, your phone gets a code you must enter too. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot get in without your phone.

## 4. Keep Your Software Updated

[[updating-your-device|Updates]] fix security holes that hackers try to break through. Turn on automatic updates so your phone and computer stay protected.

## 5. When in Doubt, Ask Someone You Trust

If something looks suspicious or you are not sure what to do, ask a family member, friend, or call a helpline. There is no shame in asking.
