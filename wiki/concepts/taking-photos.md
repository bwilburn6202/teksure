# Taking Photos

Your phone has a built-in camera that takes great pictures. Here is how to use it.

## Opening the Camera

Look for the **Camera** app on your home screen. It looks like a small camera icon. Tap it to open.

**Quick Tip:** On most phones, you can also swipe left from the lock screen to open the camera fast — no need to unlock your phone first.

## Taking a Photo

1. Point your phone at what you want to photograph.
2. Hold the phone steady with both hands.
3. Tap the big round **shutter button** at the bottom of the screen.
4. You will hear a click sound or see a flash. Your photo is saved automatically.

## Switching Between Front and Back Camera

Look for a small icon that shows two arrows in a circle, or a camera with rotating arrows. Tap it to switch between:

- **Back camera:** For taking pictures of other people, scenery, or objects.
- **Front camera:** For taking pictures of yourself (sometimes called a "selfie").

## Viewing Your Photos

- **iPhone:** Open the **Photos** app (a colorful flower icon).
- **Android:** Open the **Gallery** or **Google Photos** app.

Your most recent photos appear first. Tap any photo to see it full-size.

## Sharing a Photo

1. Open the photo you want to share.
2. Tap the **Share** button (a box with an arrow on iPhone, or three connected dots on Android).
3. Choose how you want to share it — **Text Message**, **Email**, or an app like WhatsApp.
4. Pick the person you want to send it to and tap **Send**.
