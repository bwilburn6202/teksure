# Medical Technology for Seniors

Technology can help you manage your health, communicate with your doctor, and keep track of medications — all from home.

## Patient Portals

A patient portal is a secure website or app where you can:
- **See test results** without waiting for a phone call
- **Send messages** to your doctor's office
- **Request prescription refills**
- **Schedule appointments**
- **View your medical records**

Common portals include MyChart (used by many hospitals), FollowMyHealth, and Athenahealth. Ask your doctor's office which one they use and for help signing up.

## Blood Pressure Monitors

Many blood pressure monitors now connect to your phone via Bluetooth. After taking a reading, the results are saved in an app on your phone. This creates a history your doctor can review.

Popular options: Omron, Withings, QardioArm. Look for one that is "validated" — this means it has been tested for accuracy.

## Medication Reminders

Forgetting to take medication is common. Your phone can help:
- **iPhone**: Use the built-in Health app to set medication reminders
- **Android**: Use Google Clock alarms or download Medisafe (free app)
- **Smart speakers**: "Alexa, remind me to take my medicine at 8 AM"

## Telehealth (Video Doctor Visits)

Telehealth lets you see your doctor by video call instead of going to the office. This is helpful for:
- Follow-up appointments
- Reviewing test results
- Minor illnesses (colds, rashes, questions about symptoms)
- Prescription refills

You will need: a phone, tablet, or computer with a camera, a stable internet connection, and a quiet, well-lit room.

## Fitness Trackers

Devices like Fitbit, Apple Watch, and Samsung Galaxy Watch can track:
- Steps walked each day
- Heart rate
- Sleep quality
- Reminders to move

These are not medical devices, but they can encourage healthy habits and give your doctor useful information.

## Keeping Health Data Private

- Use a strong, unique password for your patient portal
- Do not share your portal login with anyone
- Be cautious of emails asking you to "verify your medical records" — these are often scams
- Download health apps only from the official App Store or Google Play Store
