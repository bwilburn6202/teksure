# Using Email Basics

Email lets you send messages, photos, and documents to anyone with an email address. Here is how to do the basics.

## Opening Your Email

- **Gmail:** Look for the red and white envelope icon on your phone, or go to gmail.com on a computer.
- **Outlook:** Look for the blue envelope icon, or go to outlook.com.

Tap the app or go to the website and sign in with your email address and password.

## Reading an Email

Your inbox shows a list of messages. New or unread emails are usually shown in **bold**. Tap any email to open and read it.

## Writing a New Email

1. Tap the **Compose** button (it looks like a pencil or a **+** symbol).
2. In the **To** field, type the email address of the person you are sending to.
3. In the **Subject** field, type a short description of what the email is about (like "Photos from Sunday").
4. In the large text area below, type your message.
5. Tap **Send** (the arrow icon).

## Replying to an Email

Open the email you received. Tap the **Reply** button (a curved arrow pointing left). Type your response and tap **Send**.

## Adding an Attachment

An attachment is a file you send along with your email — like a photo or a document.

1. While writing an email, tap the **paperclip icon** (📎).
2. Choose the file or photo you want to attach.
3. Wait for it to upload, then send your email as normal.

## What Are CC and BCC?

- **CC (Carbon Copy):** Sends a copy of the email to another person. Everyone can see who was CC'd.
- **BCC (Blind Carbon Copy):** Sends a copy to another person, but nobody else can see their name. Use this when emailing a group and you want to keep addresses private.
