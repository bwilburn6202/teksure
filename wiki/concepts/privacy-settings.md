# Privacy Settings

## Why Privacy Settings Matter

Many apps and websites collect information about you — what you search for, where you go, what you buy. Most of this is used to show you ads, but it can also be shared or sold. Adjusting your privacy settings gives you more control over your personal information.

## What Apps and Websites Track

- **Location:** Where you are right now and where you have been.
- **Browsing history:** What websites you visit.
- **Contacts and photos:** Some apps ask for access to your entire contact list or photo library.
- **Microphone and camera:** Some apps request access even when they do not need it.
- **Ad tracking:** Companies follow your activity across apps and websites to show you targeted ads.

## Adjusting Privacy on iPhone and iPad

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Tap **Privacy & Security**.
3. Review each category (Location Services, Contacts, Photos, Microphone, Camera). Tap any category to see which apps have access and turn off anything that does not seem right.
4. To limit ad tracking: Go to **Settings > Privacy & Security > Tracking** and turn off **Allow Apps to Request to Track**.

## Adjusting Privacy on Android

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Tap **Privacy** (or **Security & Privacy** on some phones).
3. Tap **Permission Manager** to see which apps can access your location, camera, microphone, and more. Remove access for apps that do not need it.
4. To limit ad tracking: Go to **Settings > Privacy > Ads** and choose **Delete advertising ID**.

## Adjusting Privacy on Windows

1. Click the **Start** button, then **Settings**.
2. Click **Privacy & Security**.
3. Under **General**, turn off **Let apps show me personalized ads**.
4. Review **App Permissions** on the left side — you can control which apps use your camera, microphone, and location.

## App Permissions — A Good Rule of Thumb

Ask yourself: "Does this app need this access to do its job?" A weather app needs your location. A flashlight app does not need your contacts. If an app asks for something that does not make sense, deny the request.

## Location Sharing

Location sharing can be helpful (maps, ride services) but should be limited. Set most apps to **While Using the App** instead of **Always**. Turn off location access entirely for apps that have no reason to know where you are.
