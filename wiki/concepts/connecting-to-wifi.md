# Connecting to Wi-Fi

Wi-Fi lets your phone, tablet, or computer connect to the internet without using mobile data. Here is how to connect.

## On iPhone

1. Open **Settings** (the gray gear icon).
2. Tap **Wi-Fi**.
3. Make sure the Wi-Fi switch is turned **on** (green).
4. Tap the name of your home network from the list.
5. Type in your Wi-Fi password and tap **Join**.

## On Android

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Tap **Network & internet** (or **Connections**).
3. Tap **Wi-Fi** and make sure it is turned **on**.
4. Tap your network name from the list.
5. Enter the password and tap **Connect**.

## On Windows

1. Click the **Wi-Fi icon** in the bottom-right corner of your screen (it looks like a fan shape).
2. Pick your network from the list.
3. Click **Connect**, then type your password.

## How to Know You Are Connected

Look for the **Wi-Fi icon** at the top of your phone screen or the bottom-right of your computer screen. It looks like a small fan or a set of curved lines. If you see it, you are connected.

## Where to Find Your Wi-Fi Password

Your Wi-Fi password is often printed on a sticker on the bottom or back of your router — the small box your internet company gave you. It might be labeled "Network Key" or "WPA Password."

## Safety Note About Public Wi-Fi

Coffee shops, libraries, and airports often have free Wi-Fi. Be careful on public networks:

- Do not enter banking or [[password-security|password]] information.
- Do not shop online.
- If possible, wait until you are home to do anything private.
