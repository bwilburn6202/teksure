# Smartphone Basics for Beginners

## Choosing a Smartphone

- **iPhone** (Apple) — Easy to use, strong privacy, works with FaceTime
- **Android** (Samsung, Google, Motorola) — More variety in price and size

Choose the same type your family uses — they can help you learn faster.

## Making It Easier to Use

- **Increase text size** in Settings > Display > Font Size
- **Turn on Bold Text** for easier reading
- **Use Easy Mode** (Samsung) or Simple Mode (other Android) for bigger icons
- **Remove unused apps** to reduce clutter

## Five Things to Learn First

1. Making and receiving calls
2. Sending text messages
3. Taking photos
4. Connecting to WiFi
5. Downloading apps from the App Store or Play Store

## Keeping Your Phone Safe

- Lock with PIN, fingerprint, or face recognition
- Keep software updated
- Only download from official app stores
- Do not tap links in texts from unknown senders
- Back up to iCloud or Google

## Senior-Friendly Plans

- **Lively** — From $20/month with safety features
- **T-Mobile 55+** — Discounted plan for 55+
- **Consumer Cellular** — AARP-recommended, no contracts
- **Mint Mobile** — Budget option from $15/month
