# Online Banking Safety

Online banking lets you check your balance, pay bills, and transfer money from your computer or phone — without visiting a branch.

## How to Stay Safe

- **Use strong, unique passwords** — Never reuse your bank password on other sites
- **Watch for phishing emails** — Your bank will never email asking for your password or Social Security number
- **Check accounts weekly** — Review transactions and report anything suspicious immediately
- **Enable account alerts** — Get text or email notifications for every transaction

## Red Flags

- Emails/calls asking for your PIN, password, or SSN
- Texts about "locked" accounts with links to "verify" identity
- Requests to transfer money to a "safe" account
- Payment requests via gift cards, wire transfer, or cryptocurrency

## What to Do If You Suspect Fraud

1. Call your bank using the number on your card
2. Change your online banking password
3. Report to the FTC at ReportFraud.ftc.gov
4. Call AARP Fraud Watch Helpline: 877-908-3360
