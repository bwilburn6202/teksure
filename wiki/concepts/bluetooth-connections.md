# Bluetooth Connections

Bluetooth is a short-range wireless technology that lets devices talk to each other without cables. It works within about 30 feet (10 meters) — roughly the size of a room.

## What Uses Bluetooth

- **Wireless headphones and earbuds** — AirPods, Beats, JBL, Sony
- **Hearing aids** — many modern hearing aids connect to your phone via Bluetooth for phone calls and streaming
- **Car audio** — play music and take phone calls through your car speakers
- **Wireless speakers** — portable speakers like JBL, Bose, Amazon Echo
- **Fitness trackers** — Fitbit, Apple Watch sync data to your phone
- **Wireless keyboards and mice** — connect to your computer without cables

## How Pairing Works

"Pairing" means introducing two Bluetooth devices to each other for the first time. Once paired, they will reconnect automatically in the future.

1. **Turn on Bluetooth** on your phone or computer (usually in Settings).
2. **Put the other device in pairing mode** — this is different for each device. For headphones, you usually hold the power button until a light flashes. Check the manual.
3. **Find the device** in your phone's Bluetooth settings list and tap it.
4. **Confirm the connection** if prompted.

## Troubleshooting Bluetooth Problems

- **Cannot find the device** — make sure it is in pairing mode (light should be flashing). Restart both devices and try again.
- **Keeps disconnecting** — move closer. Bluetooth works best within 30 feet. Walls and other electronics can interfere.
- **Audio sounds choppy** — check battery levels on both devices. Low battery can cause audio problems.
- **Paired to the wrong device** — go to Bluetooth settings, tap the device name, and choose "Forget" or "Unpair." Then pair again to the correct device.

## Bluetooth vs Wi-Fi

Bluetooth and Wi-Fi are both wireless, but they do different things:
- **Bluetooth** connects two devices directly (phone to headphones). Short range.
- **Wi-Fi** connects devices to the internet through a router. Longer range.

They do not interfere with each other — you can use both at the same time.
