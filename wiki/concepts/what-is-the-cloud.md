# What Is the Cloud?

You have probably heard people say "it's in the cloud." That can sound confusing, but the idea is straightforward.

## The Cloud in Plain Language

The cloud is a safe place on the internet where your files, photos, and data are stored. Think of it like a **digital filing cabinet** that follows you everywhere. You can open that cabinet from your phone, tablet, or computer — as long as you have an internet connection.

It is not magic. "The cloud" is made up of real computers sitting in large buildings called data centers. Companies like Apple, Google, and Microsoft own these buildings and rent out storage space to you.

## Common Cloud Services

- **iCloud** — Made by Apple. Comes with every iPhone, iPad, and Mac. Stores your photos, contacts, and device backups.
- **Google Drive** — Made by Google. Comes with every Gmail account. Stores documents, photos (through Google Photos), and more.
- **OneDrive** — Made by Microsoft. Comes with Windows and Microsoft 365. Stores documents, photos, and files.

Most of these give you some free storage space. You can pay for more if you need it.

## Why Use the Cloud?

- **Backup:** If your phone breaks or gets lost, your photos and contacts are still safe in the cloud.
- **Access anywhere:** Look at your files from any device — phone, tablet, or computer.
- **Free up space:** Move old photos and files off your device to [[freeing-up-storage|free up storage]] without losing them.

## Is the Cloud Safe?

Your cloud files are protected by your account password. To stay safe, use a strong [[password-security|password]] and turn on [[staying-safe-online|two-factor authentication]].
