# Printing Basics

A printer lets you create paper copies of documents, photos, emails, and more. Modern printers connect to your computer, phone, or tablet either by a USB cable or wirelessly over Wi-Fi.

## Types of Printers

- **Inkjet printers** use liquid ink. Good for photos and color printing. Ink cartridges need replacing every few months.
- **Laser printers** use toner (a fine powder). Best for text documents. Toner lasts longer than ink.
- **All-in-one printers** can also scan, copy, and sometimes fax. A good choice if you want one device that does everything.

## USB vs Wireless Printing

**USB printers** connect with a cable directly to your computer. They are reliable but only work with the device they are plugged into.

**Wireless (Wi-Fi) printers** connect to your home network. Any device on the same network can print to them — your computer, phone, or tablet. Most new printers are wireless.

**AirPrint** is Apple's wireless printing feature. If your printer supports AirPrint and is on the same Wi-Fi network as your iPhone or iPad, you can print without installing anything extra.

## Common Printer Problems

- **"Printer offline"** — Restart the printer, check it is connected to Wi-Fi, and make sure it is set as your default printer.
- **Paper jam** — Open the printer cover, gently remove the stuck paper, and check for small torn pieces.
- **Faded or blurry prints** — Your ink or toner may be running low. Check the ink levels in your printer settings.
- **Cannot find the printer** — Make sure your printer and your device are on the same Wi-Fi network.

## When to Replace Ink

Most printers show a low-ink warning on your computer or on the printer's small screen. You can buy replacement cartridges online or at office supply stores. Look for the cartridge model number printed on your current cartridge or in your printer's manual.
