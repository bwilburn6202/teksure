# Making Phone Calls

Your smartphone can do a lot, but at its core it is still a phone. Here is how to make and receive calls.

## Finding the Phone App

Look for a green icon that looks like an old-fashioned telephone handset. On iPhone and Android, it is usually at the bottom of your home screen. Tap it to open.

## Dialing a Number

1. Open the **Phone** app.
2. Tap the **Keypad** tab (it looks like a grid of numbers).
3. Type in the phone number.
4. Tap the green **Call** button.

## Using Your Contacts

Instead of typing a number every time, you can save people in your **Contacts**.

1. Open the **Phone** app.
2. Tap the **Contacts** tab.
3. Find the person you want to call.
4. Tap their name, then tap the phone number to call them.

**Quick Tip:** Add people you call often to your **Favorites** list. In the Phone app, tap the **Favorites** tab, then tap the **+** button to add someone.

## Answering a Call

When someone calls you, a screen will pop up with their name or number.

- **iPhone:** Swipe the green button to the right, or tap **Accept** if the phone is unlocked.
- **Android:** Swipe the green phone icon up, or tap **Answer**.

## Using Speakerphone

During a call, look for the **Speaker** button on your screen. Tap it to turn speakerphone on. This lets you hear the other person without holding the phone to your ear. Tap it again to turn it off.

## Ending a Call

Tap the red **End Call** button on your screen. It is usually a red circle or red phone icon at the bottom.
