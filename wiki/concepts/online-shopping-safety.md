# Online Shopping Safety

## Shopping Online Can Be Safe — With the Right Habits

Buying things online is convenient and often less expensive than shopping in stores. Millions of people do it every day without any trouble. The key is knowing what to watch for.

## Trusted Shopping Sites

- **Amazon.com** — The largest online store. Look for items marked "Ships from and sold by Amazon" for the most reliable experience.
- **Walmart.com, Target.com** — Major retailers with good return policies.
- **eBay.com** — Good for new and used items. Always check the **seller's rating** (look for sellers with 95% or higher positive feedback).

## How to Tell If a Website Is Safe

- **Look for the lock icon** in your browser's address bar. This means the site uses encryption to protect your information.
- **Check the web address.** It should start with **https://** (the "s" stands for secure).
- **Be cautious of unfamiliar sites** with prices that seem too good to be true. If a brand-new TV is listed for $50, something is wrong.
- **Search for reviews.** Before buying from a new site, search for "[site name] reviews" or "[site name] scam" to see what others say.

## Secure Checkout Tips

- **Use a credit card** instead of a debit card. Credit cards have stronger fraud protection — you can dispute charges more easily.
- **Never pay with gift cards or wire transfers.** Legitimate businesses do not ask for this.
- **Do not save your credit card information** on websites you do not use often.
- **Check your bank statement** after shopping to make sure you were charged the right amount.

## Checking Seller Ratings

On Amazon and eBay, every seller has a rating based on past customers' experiences. Before buying:

1. Click on the seller's name.
2. Look at their star rating and read recent reviews.
3. Avoid sellers with very few ratings or many complaints.

## What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

- **Item never arrived:** Contact the seller first. If they do not respond, open a dispute through the website (Amazon, eBay, and others all have buyer protection programs).
- **Charged for something you did not order:** Call your credit card company and request a chargeback.
- **You gave information to a fake site:** Change your passwords immediately, monitor your bank accounts, and report the site at **ReportFraud.ftc.gov**.
