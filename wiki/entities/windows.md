# Windows 10 and 11

Windows is the operating system made by **Microsoft** that runs on most desktop and laptop computers. The two versions you will see today are **Windows 10** and **Windows 11**. They work the same way in most respects — Windows 11 has a more modern look with the Start menu centered at the bottom of the screen.

## Key Parts of Windows

- **Start Menu** — Click the Windows icon (bottom-left on Windows 10, bottom-center on Windows 11) to find your apps, settings, and power options.
- **File Explorer** — The yellow folder icon. Use it to browse your documents, pictures, and downloads.
- **Settings** — Click Start, then the gear icon. This is where you change Wi-Fi, display, sound, and account options.
- **Taskbar** — The bar along the bottom of your screen with pinned apps and the clock.

## Common Tasks

- **Install updates:** Settings > Windows Update > Check for Updates. Keeping Windows updated protects you from security problems.
- **Take a screenshot:** Press the Windows key + Shift + S to select an area of your screen.
- **Open Task Manager:** Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc. Task Manager shows what programs are running and lets you close ones that are stuck.
- **Restart your computer:** Click Start > Power > Restart. This fixes many common issues.

## Common Problems and Fixes

- **Computer running slow:** Restart it, check for updates, and close programs you are not using.
- **Wi-Fi not connecting:** Click the Wi-Fi icon in the taskbar, turn Wi-Fi off and back on, then reconnect.
- **Program not responding:** Open Task Manager, select the stuck program, and click "End Task."
