# Microsoft Services

Microsoft makes Windows, but it also offers a set of online services that work together through your **Microsoft account**. If you have an @outlook.com, @hotmail.com, or @live.com email address, you already have a Microsoft account.

## Core Services

- **Outlook** — Email, calendar, and contacts. Access it at outlook.com or through the Outlook app. Outlook replaces the older Hotmail.
- **OneDrive** — Cloud storage (5 GB free). Save your files online and access them from any device. OneDrive is built into Windows.
- **Microsoft 365** — The paid version of Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and more. A free online version is available at microsoft365.com with limited features.
- **Microsoft Teams** — Video calls, group chat, and file sharing. Commonly used by workplaces and schools.
- **Microsoft Edge** — The web browser that comes with Windows. It can sync bookmarks and passwords across devices.

## How They Connect

One Microsoft account ties everything together. Files you save in OneDrive appear in File Explorer on your Windows PC. Outlook calendar events can include Teams meeting links. Word documents can be saved directly to OneDrive.

## Free vs. Paid

You can use Outlook email, OneDrive (5 GB), and the online versions of Word, Excel, and PowerPoint for free. **Microsoft 365** (starting around $7/month) gives you the full desktop apps, 1 TB of OneDrive storage, and advanced features.
