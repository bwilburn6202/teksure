# Common Scam Types

Scammers use many tricks to steal your money or personal information. Below is a list of the most common scams, with a short description and the main warning sign (red flag) for each.

## Phone and Computer Scams

- **Tech support scam** — Someone calls or a pop-up says your computer has a virus and asks you to pay for "repairs." *Red flag: Real companies do not call you out of the blue about viruses.*
- **Computer pop-up scam** — A full-screen warning appears saying your computer is locked. *Red flag: Real warnings never include a phone number to call.*
- **IRS/tax scam** — A caller claims you owe taxes and threatens arrest. *Red flag: The IRS contacts you by mail first, never by phone threats.*
- **SSN suspension scam** — A call says your Social Security number has been "suspended." *Red flag: Your SSN cannot be suspended.*
- **Utility bill scam** — A caller says your power will be shut off unless you pay immediately. *Red flag: Utilities send written notices before disconnecting service.*
- **AI voice clone scam** — A call sounds like a family member asking for money. *Red flag: Hang up and call that person directly on their real number.*

## Email and Text Scams

- **Phishing email** — A fake email that looks like it comes from a real company, asking you to click a link or share your password. *Red flag: The sender address does not match the real company.*
- **Bank text scam** — A text says there is a problem with your bank account and includes a link. *Red flag: Your bank will not ask for your password by text.*
- **Package delivery scam** — A text says a package could not be delivered and asks you to click a link. *Red flag: Check tracking directly on the carrier's official website.*
- **QR code scam** — A fake QR code on a flyer, parking meter, or email sends you to a scam website. *Red flag: Do not scan QR codes from unknown sources.*

## Money and Identity Scams

- **Lottery/prize scam** — You "won" a contest you never entered, but must pay a fee to claim it. *Red flag: Real prizes never require upfront payment.*
- **Gift card scam** — Someone tells you to buy gift cards and read the numbers over the phone as "payment." *Red flag: No real business or government agency accepts gift cards as payment.*
- **Investment scam** — Promises of guaranteed high returns with no risk. *Red flag: All investments carry risk — guaranteed returns do not exist.*
- **Mortgage/debt relief scam** — An offer to lower your mortgage or erase debt for an upfront fee. *Red flag: Legitimate programs do not charge fees before providing help.*
- **Shopping scam** — A website or social media ad offers popular items at unbelievably low prices. *Red flag: Prices far below retail and no verifiable contact information.*

## Relationship and Emotional Scams

- **Romance scam** — Someone you meet online builds a relationship, then asks for money. *Red flag: They always have excuses for why they cannot video call or meet.*
- **Grandparent scam** — A caller pretends to be your grandchild in trouble and begs you to send money. *Red flag: Hang up and call your grandchild directly.*
- **Charity scam** — A fake charity pressures you to donate, especially after a disaster. *Red flag: The caller pressures you to give right now and will not send written information.*
- **Dating app scam** — Someone on a dating app quickly moves the conversation off the app, then asks for money or personal details. *Red flag: They push to move to another messaging platform very fast.*

## Travel and Event Scams

- **ESTA/visa scam** — A fake website charges a large fee for a government travel form that costs much less on the official site. *Red flag: The official ESTA website is esta.cbp.dhs.gov — any other site is not legitimate.*
- **Event ticket scam** — Fake tickets for concerts, sports, or events sold through social media or unofficial sites. *Red flag: The seller cannot provide proof of purchase or uses a non-refundable payment method.*

## What to Do If You Suspect a Scam

1. **Stop** — Do not send money, click links, or share personal information.
2. **Verify** — Contact the real company or person using a number you already trust.
3. **Report** — See [[us-resources]] for where to report scams.
