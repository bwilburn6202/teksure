## Guide 1: How to Set Up and Use Gmail

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-gmail`
**Category:** `essential-skills`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 7 min
**Tags:** gmail, google, email, setup, account
**Emoji:** 📧

### Excerpt
Create a free Gmail account and learn how to send, receive, and organize your emails from any device.

### Steps

1. **Go to Gmail**
   - On a computer, open your browser and go to gmail.com. On a phone, the Gmail app is usually pre-installed on Android; on iPhone, download it from the App Store.

2. **Create a Google account**
   - Click "Create account." Enter your first and last name. Choose an email address — try your name or initials (for example, jane.smith@gmail.com). If your first choice is taken, Google will suggest alternatives.

3. **Set a strong password**
   - Create a password with at least 12 characters. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
   - *Tip:* Write your email address and password down and keep it in a safe place until you have memorized them.

4. **Add recovery information**
   - Google will ask for a phone number and a backup email. These are used to recover your account if you ever forget your password. Add them — it could save you a major headache later.

5. **Send your first email**
   - Click "Compose" (the big button with a pencil icon). Enter the recipient's email address in the "To" field. Add a subject line. Type your message. Click "Send."

6. **Read and reply to emails**
   - Emails appear in your "Inbox." Click on any email to read it. Click "Reply" at the bottom to respond.

7. **Organize with labels and folders**
   - Gmail uses "labels" instead of folders. Click the three dots on an email and choose "Label as" to categorize your emails (Bills, Family, Medical, etc.).
   - *Tip:* Use the search bar at the top to find old emails. Type a person's name, a subject, or a keyword.

8. **Understand spam**
   - Gmail automatically filters suspicious emails into the "Spam" folder. Check it occasionally — sometimes real emails end up there by mistake.
   - *Warning:* Never open attachments or click links from senders you do not recognize.

## Guide 2: How to Set Up and Use Outlook Email (Hotmail)

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-outlook-email`
**Category:** `essential-skills`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** outlook, hotmail, microsoft, email, setup
**Emoji:** 📬

### Excerpt
Create a free Outlook.com email account (formerly Hotmail) and learn how to send messages, add attachments, and keep your inbox organized.

### Steps

1. **Go to Outlook.com**
   - Open your browser and go to outlook.com. Click "Create free account."

2. **Choose your email address**
   - Pick a username (like your name) and choose @outlook.com or @hotmail.com. Then create a strong password.

3. **Verify your identity**
   - Microsoft will ask for your phone number or an alternate email to verify your identity and help you recover your account if needed.

4. **Explore your inbox**
   - Your inbox is organized into "Focused" (important emails) and "Other" (newsletters and less important items). You can switch between them using the tabs at the top.

5. **Send an email**
   - Click "New mail" at the top left. Enter the recipient's email address, add a subject, and type your message. Click "Send."

6. **Add an attachment**
   - When composing an email, click the paperclip icon. Choose a file from your computer (like a photo or document). Wait for it to upload, then send.
   - *Tip:* If the file is too large, Outlook will offer to upload it to OneDrive and send a link instead.

7. **Create folders**
   - Right-click on "Folders" in the left sidebar and choose "Create new folder." Name it something useful like "Medical" or "Receipts." Drag emails into folders to keep organized.

### Official Sources

## Guide 3: How to Set Up and Use Yahoo Mail

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-yahoo-mail`
**Category:** `essential-skills`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** yahoo, email, setup, account
**Emoji:** ✉️

### Excerpt
Create a free Yahoo Mail account and learn how to manage your inbox — Yahoo Mail is one of the oldest and most trusted email services.

### Steps

1. **Go to Yahoo Mail**
   - Open your browser and go to mail.yahoo.com. Click "Create an account."

2. **Fill in your information**
   - Enter your name, choose an email address, create a password, and add your phone number for account recovery.

3. **Verify your phone number**
   - Yahoo will send a text message with a code. Enter the code to verify your account.

4. **Send an email**
   - Click "Compose" in the upper left. Enter the recipient's address, add a subject line, type your message, and click "Send."

5. **Organize your inbox**
   - Use the built-in folders: Inbox, Sent, Drafts, Spam, and Trash. You can create custom folders by clicking "New Folder" at the bottom of the folder list.

6. **Use the search feature**
   - The search bar at the top helps you find old emails quickly. Search by sender name, subject, or keywords.

7. **Secure your account**
   - Go to Account Security in your settings and enable two-step verification. This sends a code to your phone each time you sign in from a new device.
   - *Warning:* Yahoo had major data breaches in the past. Use a unique, strong password and enable two-step verification.

### Official Sources
- [Yahoo Mail Help](https://help.yahoo.com/kb/mail)

## Guide 4: How to Manage Email Attachments (Photos, Documents, Files)

**Slug:** `how-to-manage-email-attachments`
**Category:** `essential-skills`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** email, attachments, photos, documents, download
**Emoji:** 📎

### Excerpt
Learn how to open, download, and send attachments in any email service — photos, documents, and more.

### Steps

1. **Recognize an attachment**
   - Emails with attachments show a paperclip icon or a preview of the attached file (like a photo thumbnail or document name).

2. **Open an attachment**
   - Click on the attachment name or thumbnail to preview it. Most email services let you view photos, PDFs, and documents without downloading them.

3. **Download an attachment**
   - Click the download icon (usually a downward arrow) to save the file to your computer or phone. On a computer, it goes to your "Downloads" folder. On a phone, it saves to "Files" or "Downloads."
   - *Warning:* Never download attachments from senders you do not recognize. These can contain viruses or malware.

4. **Send an attachment**
   - When writing a new email, click the paperclip icon. Browse your files and select what you want to attach. Wait for it to upload before sending.
   - *Tip:* Most email services limit attachments to 25 MB. For larger files (like videos), use a sharing service like Google Drive or Dropbox.

5. **Send a photo from your phone**
   - In the Gmail or Outlook app, tap the paperclip or attachment icon when composing. Choose "Photo" or "Gallery" and select the photos you want to send.

6. **Be cautious with unfamiliar file types**
   - Safe file types: .pdf, .jpg, .png, .docx. Be cautious with: .exe, .zip, .bat — these can contain harmful software.
   - *Warning:* If you receive an unexpected .zip or .exe file, even from someone you know, contact them separately to confirm they actually sent it.
