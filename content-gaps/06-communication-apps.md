## Guide 1: How to Set Up and Use WhatsApp

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-whatsapp`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 6 min
**Tags:** whatsapp, messaging, video-call, family, international
**Emoji:** 💬

### Excerpt
Send free text messages, photos, and make free video calls to family and friends around the world using WhatsApp.

### Steps

1. **Download WhatsApp**
   - Open the App Store (iPhone) or Google Play Store (Android). Search for "WhatsApp Messenger" and tap "Install."

2. **Open and verify your phone number**
   - Open WhatsApp and enter your phone number. WhatsApp will send a code via text message. Enter the code to verify.

3. **Set up your profile**
   - Add your name and a profile photo so your contacts can recognize you.

4. **Start a chat**
   - Tap the new message icon (pencil icon or chat bubble). WhatsApp will show contacts who also use WhatsApp. Tap a name to start a conversation.
   - *Tip:* WhatsApp uses your phone's contact list. Anyone in your contacts who has WhatsApp will appear automatically.

5. **Send photos and videos**
   - In a chat, tap the paperclip icon (Android) or "+" icon (iPhone). Choose "Gallery" to send a saved photo, or "Camera" to take a new one.

6. **Make a voice or video call**
   - Open a chat with someone and tap the phone icon (voice call) or video camera icon (video call) at the top. These calls are free over Wi-Fi or data — even international calls.
   - *Tip:* WhatsApp calls to other countries are free. This is one of the best ways to stay in touch with family abroad.

7. **Create a group chat**
   - Tap "New Group," select the contacts you want to add, and give the group a name (like "Family" or "Book Club"). Everyone in the group can send messages and share photos.

8. **Understand privacy**
   - WhatsApp messages are "end-to-end encrypted," meaning only you and the person you are messaging can read them — not even WhatsApp.
   - *Warning:* Be cautious of messages from unknown numbers. Scammers use WhatsApp too. Never share personal or financial information with strangers.

## Guide 2: How to Set Up and Use Telegram

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-telegram`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** telegram, messaging, group-chat, channels, privacy
**Emoji:** ✈️

### Excerpt
Set up Telegram for fast, private messaging with individuals and groups — great for family chats, community groups, and news channels.

### Steps

1. **Download Telegram**
   - Search for "Telegram" in the App Store or Google Play Store. It is free.

2. **Sign up with your phone number**
   - Enter your phone number. Telegram will send a verification code via text.

3. **Set your name and username**
   - Enter your first name. You can also create a @username so people can find you without knowing your phone number.

4. **Find and message contacts**
   - Telegram checks your phone contacts. Anyone using Telegram will appear in your contacts list. Tap a name to start chatting.

5. **Join channels and groups**
   - Channels are like newsletters — organizations post updates and you read them. Groups are conversations with multiple people. Search for topics you are interested in using the search bar.
   - *Tip:* Many churches, community organizations, and local groups use Telegram for announcements.

6. **Use Telegram on your computer**
   - Go to desktop.telegram.org to download Telegram for your computer. Your messages sync across all your devices.

7. **Understand privacy settings**
   - In Settings → Privacy and Security, you can control who sees your phone number, profile photo, and online status. Telegram also offers "Secret Chats" with extra encryption.

### Official Sources
- [Telegram FAQ](https://telegram.org/faq)

## Guide 3: How to Set Up and Use Signal for Private Messaging

**Slug:** `how-to-set-up-signal`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 4 min
**Tags:** signal, privacy, encrypted-messaging, secure-communication
**Emoji:** 🔒

### Excerpt
Signal is the most private messaging app available — recommended by security experts for personal conversations you want to keep confidential.

### Steps

1. **Download Signal**
   - Search for "Signal" in the App Store or Google Play Store. It is free and made by a nonprofit.

2. **Register with your phone number**
   - Enter your phone number and the verification code sent to you.

3. **Set up your profile**
   - Add your name and optionally a photo. This is visible to your contacts.

4. **Start messaging**
   - Signal checks your contacts for other Signal users. Tap a name to start a conversation. You can send text, photos, voice messages, and files.

5. **Make encrypted calls**
   - Tap the phone or video icon to make a call. All Signal calls are fully encrypted.

6. **Set disappearing messages**
   - In any chat, tap the contact's name at the top, then turn on "Disappearing Messages." Choose how long messages should last before they automatically delete (from 30 seconds to 4 weeks).
   - *Tip:* Disappearing messages are great for sensitive conversations — like sharing temporary passwords or medical information.

7. **Use Signal on your computer**
   - Download the Signal desktop app at signal.org. Link it to your phone by scanning a QR code.

### Official Sources
- [Signal Support](https://support.signal.org)

## Guide 4: How to Use FaceTime on iPhone and iPad

**Slug:** `how-to-use-facetime`
**Category:** `app-guides`
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Read Time:** 5 min
**Tags:** facetime, video-call, apple, iphone, ipad, family
**Emoji:** 📹

### Excerpt
Make free video calls to friends and family using FaceTime — it comes built into every iPhone, iPad, and Mac.

### Steps

1. **Open FaceTime**
   - Find the green FaceTime icon on your home screen and tap it. If you do not see it, search by swiping down on your home screen and typing "FaceTime."

2. **Start a video call**
   - Tap the "New FaceTime" button. Type a contact name, phone number, or email address. Then tap the green video camera button.
   - *Tip:* You can FaceTime anyone who has an Apple device — iPhone, iPad, or Mac. You can also invite Android and Windows users via a link.

3. **Answer a FaceTime call**
   - When someone FaceTimes you, a notification appears. Tap the green "Accept" button to answer.

4. **Switch cameras**
   - During a call, tap the flip camera icon to switch between the front camera (your face) and the back camera (what you are looking at).

5. **Make a group FaceTime call**
   - Tap "New FaceTime" and add multiple people. You can video call with up to 32 people at once.
   - *Tip:* Group FaceTime is great for family reunions, birthday celebrations, and holiday calls.

6. **Use FaceTime Audio**
   - If you prefer a voice-only call (or your internet is slow), tap the phone icon instead of the video icon when starting a call.

7. **Send a FaceTime link to Android users**
   - Tap "Create Link" at the top of the FaceTime screen. Share this link via text or email. The other person can join from any web browser — they do not need an Apple device.

### Official Sources
- [Apple: Use FaceTime](https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT204380)
